<Step title="Two reasons food matters" kind="explain">

We eat because we're hungry — but hunger is just the signal. Underneath it, the body is making a very precise request: **give me energy and give me building materials**.

Every cell in your body runs on chemical energy, and the only way to get it is from food. Without enough energy, your heart slows, your brain fogs, your muscles fail. But food is more than fuel — the atoms in the food you eat literally become *you*. The proteins in last week's meals are now enzymes in your gut, antibodies in your blood, and fibres in your muscles.

So when you eat, you're doing two separate but equally important things:
- **Fuelling** ongoing processes — staying warm, moving, thinking, breathing.
- **Building and maintaining** structures — replacing worn-out cells, healing tissue, making hormones.

</Step>

<Step title="What is a calorie?" kind="explain">

A **calorie** (technically a kilocalorie, kcal) is simply a unit of energy — the amount of heat needed to raise one litre of water by 1 °C. Food scientists measure it by burning food in a device called a bomb calorimeter and catching the released heat.

Your body is not a bonfire, but the chemistry is analogous: controlled oxidation in your cells releases energy from food, and that energy powers everything from a heartbeat to a sprint.

- Carbohydrates provide **4 kcal per gram**.
- Proteins provide **4 kcal per gram**.
- Fats provide **9 kcal per gram** — more than twice as energy-dense.
- Alcohol provides 7 kcal per gram (but is not a nutrient).

<Callout type="info">
Kilojoules (kJ) are the metric unit for the same thing. 1 kcal ≈ 4.18 kJ. UK and Australian food labels often list both; US labels use kcal only (labelled just "Calories" with a capital C).
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="The building blocks" kind="explain">

Beyond energy, the body needs specific molecules it can't always make from scratch:

- **Amino acids** from dietary protein → used to build every enzyme, antibody, structural protein, and hormone.
- **Fatty acids** from dietary fat → cell membrane components, brain tissue, hormone precursors.
- **Vitamins and minerals** → co-factors for thousands of biochemical reactions; cannot be synthesised in adequate amounts by the body.
- **Water** → the solvent and transport medium for virtually everything in biology.
- **Fibre** → not strictly a "nutrient" (we can't digest it), but it feeds gut bacteria and keeps digestion working.

A diet that provides enough energy but lacks these building materials causes **deficiency diseases** — scurvy (vitamin C), rickets (vitamin D), anaemia (iron or B12), and many more.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- Eating serves two purposes: **energy** for all body processes, and **building materials** for growth and repair.
- Energy in food is measured in **kilocalories (kcal)**; carbs and protein give 4 kcal/g, fat gives 9 kcal/g.
- Essential nutrients — amino acids, fatty acids, vitamins, minerals — cannot be made by the body in sufficient amounts.
- **Energy balance** (in vs out) determines body weight over time; small daily gaps compound into significant changes.

</Step>
