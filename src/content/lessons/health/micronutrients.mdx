<Step title="The nutrients you need in tiny amounts" kind="explain">

Your body needs food for two big reasons. First, **fuel** — carbohydrates, fats, and proteins (macronutrients) provide the energy and building material for tissue. Second, **control** — vitamins and minerals (micronutrients) make all those processes actually work.

The word *micro* does not mean unimportant. It just means you need them in milligrams or micrograms rather than grams. Without them, the most energy-rich diet in the world still breaks down:

- Enzymes cannot catalyse reactions without the right mineral cofactors.
- Cells cannot divide without folate and B12 to copy DNA.
- Wounds do not heal without Vitamin C to build collagen.
- Bones stay soft without Vitamin D to absorb calcium.

Think of macronutrients as the raw materials on a building site and micronutrients as the skilled workers — you can have mountains of bricks, but without the workers nothing gets built.

</Step>

<Step title="Vitamins vs minerals — what's the difference?" kind="explain">

Both are essential micronutrients, but they differ in origin and chemistry.

**Vitamins** are organic compounds — they contain carbon and were made by a living thing (a plant, animal, or bacterium). Because they are organic, they can be destroyed by heat, light, or oxygen. There are 13 recognised vitamins.

**Minerals** are inorganic elements — they come straight from the soil and water. Plants absorb them from soil; animals (including us) absorb them from food. They cannot be destroyed by cooking.

Vitamins split further into two groups:

- **Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K)** — absorbed with dietary fat, stored in body fat and the liver. Because they accumulate, excessive doses can reach toxic levels.
- **Water-soluble vitamins (B-group, C)** — dissolve in water, are not stored in large amounts, and excess is excreted in urine. But "you'll just pee it out" is not a licence for megadosing — very high doses of some B vitamins can still cause nerve damage.

<Callout type="info">
A quick memory trick: **ADEK** are fat-soluble (the word "ADEK" contains no vowels other than A — fat stores are what sticks around). Everything else (all the Bs and C) is water-soluble.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Micronutrients** — vitamins and minerals — are needed in tiny amounts but are essential for every major body process.
- **Vitamins** are organic (carbon-containing); **minerals** are inorganic elements absorbed from soil and water.
- **Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K)** are stored in body fat and can build to toxic levels with excess supplementation.
- **Water-soluble vitamins (B-group, C)** are largely excreted in urine, but high doses can still cause harm.
- A deficiency in even one micronutrient — despite a high-calorie diet — can cause serious disease.
