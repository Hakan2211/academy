<Step title="One field, many questions" kind="explain">

Philosophy is huge, but it isn't a shapeless blob. Over the centuries it has organised itself into a handful of **branches**, each defined by one central question. Knowing the map keeps you oriented: when a question puzzles you, you can ask *which branch is this?*

The four classic pillars are **logic**, **epistemology**, **metaphysics**, and **ethics** — with **aesthetics** and **political philosophy** rounding out the family.

</Step>

Notice how the branches connect. You can't decide *how to live* (ethics) without some view of *what's real* (metaphysics) and *what you can know* (epistemology) — and all of it depends on reasoning well (logic). They're less like separate rooms and more like rooms in one house.

</Step>

<Step title="The four pillars in a sentence each" kind="explain">

A quick way to keep them straight:

- **Logic** — the study of *good reasoning*. What makes an argument valid? It's the toolkit every other branch uses.
- **Epistemology** — the theory of *knowledge*. What can we know, and how do we tell knowledge from mere opinion?
- **Metaphysics** — the study of *reality* itself. What exists? Are we free? What is time, mind, or cause?
- **Ethics** — the study of *how to live*. What is right, good, and just?

Add **aesthetics** (beauty and art) and **political philosophy** (how we should live *together*), and you have the full sweep. Every philosopher you'll meet — Socrates, Kant, Nietzsche — worked in some combination of these.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- Philosophy is organised into **branches**, each defined by a central question.
- The four pillars: **logic** (good reasoning), **epistemology** (knowledge), **metaphysics** (reality), **ethics** (how to live).
- **Aesthetics** (beauty/art) and **political philosophy** (living together) complete the family.
- The branches interlock — your ethics depends on your metaphysics and epistemology, and all of it rests on logic.

</Step>
