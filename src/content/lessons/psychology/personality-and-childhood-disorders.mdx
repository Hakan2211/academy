<Step title="Patterns that run deep — or start early" kind="explain">

This lesson gathers three families that don't fit the "an alarm went off" or "mood crashed" mould. Some involve **long-standing patterns** woven into how a person experiences the world and relates to others. Others **begin in childhood**, shaping development from the start. Each is widely misunderstood, so we'll go gently and accurately.

</Step>

<Step title="Personality disorders" kind="explain">

A **personality disorder** is an enduring, inflexible pattern of inner experience and behaviour that departs markedly from cultural expectations, shows up across many situations, and causes distress or difficulty — usually traceable back to adolescence or early adulthood. Two are especially important to understand clearly:

- **Borderline personality disorder (BPD)** — marked by intense instability: stormy relationships, a shifting sense of self, powerful and rapidly changing emotions, fear of abandonment, and impulsivity. Behind much of it is deep emotional pain, often rooted in early trauma or invalidation. It is treatable — therapies like DBT help many people substantially — and people with BPD deserve compassion, not the dismissive labels they too often receive.
- **Antisocial personality disorder (ASPD)** — a persistent pattern of disregard for others' rights: deceit, impulsivity, irresponsibility, and a lack of remorse. Importantly, *most* people with ASPD are not violent, and the diagnosis is not a synonym for "criminal" or "evil" — it describes a pattern of relating, with its own developmental roots.

</Step>

<Step title="Eating disorders" kind="explain">

**Eating disorders** are serious — and, crucially, are *not* about vanity or willpower. They involve severe disturbances in eating behaviour and a distressing, distorted relationship with food, weight, and body image:

- **Anorexia nervosa** — restriction of food leading to significantly low body weight, an intense fear of gaining weight, and a distorted body image. It has one of the highest mortality rates of any psychological disorder.
- **Bulimia nervosa** — cycles of binge eating followed by compensatory behaviour (such as purging or excessive exercise), often with body weight in a normal range, hidden by shame.
- **Binge-eating disorder** — recurrent episodes of eating large amounts with a sense of loss of control, without regular compensatory behaviour.

These conditions sit at the meeting point of biology, psychology, and culture (including intense social pressure around body image), and they are treatable — early help matters greatly.

</Step>

<Step title="Childhood-onset (neurodevelopmental) conditions" kind="explain">

Some conditions are **neurodevelopmental** — differences in how the brain develops that appear early and shape attention, learning, or social communication across life. Two of the most common deserve careful, respectful framing:

- **ADHD (attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder)** — persistent patterns of inattention and/or hyperactivity-impulsivity that interfere with functioning, present from childhood. It reflects real differences in brain systems governing attention and self-regulation — not laziness or bad parenting.
- **Autism spectrum disorder (ASD)** — differences in social communication and interaction alongside focused interests and a preference for routine, often with distinctive sensory experiences. Today it's understood as a **spectrum** and increasingly through the lens of **neurodiversity**: a different way of processing the world, with genuine strengths as well as challenges, not simply a deficit.

Language matters here too — these are people with ADHD or autistic people, described in ways they themselves recognise and accept.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Personality disorders** are enduring, inflexible patterns: **BPD** (emotional instability, fear of abandonment, often rooted in trauma — and treatable) and **ASPD** (disregard for others' rights — *not* a synonym for "criminal").
- **Eating disorders** — **anorexia** (very low weight, high mortality), **bulimia** (binge-purge cycles), **binge-eating disorder** — are serious illnesses, *not* vanity or willpower.
- **Neurodevelopmental** conditions begin early: **ADHD** (inattention/hyperactivity from real brain differences) and **autism (ASD)**, understood as a **spectrum** and through **neurodiversity**.
- Respectful, person-first (or identity-affirming) **language** matters throughout.
- Together they show how broad "disorder" is — from birth-onset to early adulthood — defeating any single picture of mental illness.

</Step>
