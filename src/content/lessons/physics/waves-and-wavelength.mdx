<Step title="Energy that travels, matter that stays" kind="explain">

Drop a pebble in a pond and ripples race outward — but a floating leaf just bobs up and down; it doesn't get carried to the shore. That's the secret of every **wave**:

> A wave transports **energy** from place to place **without transporting matter**.

The water (or air, or string) only wobbles in place. It's the *disturbance* that travels.

</Step>

<Step title="Two ways to wobble" kind="interactive">

There are two basic kinds of wave, and the difference is *which way the medium moves* compared to the wave's travel. Toggle between them:

<WaveType />

- **Transverse** — the medium moves *across* the direction of travel (light, a flicked rope).
- **Longitudinal** — the medium moves *along* it, bunching into compressions (sound).

</Step>

<Step title="The anatomy of a wave" kind="explain">

Every wave has a few key measurements:

- **Crest** — a high point; **trough** — a low point.
- **Amplitude (A)** — how far the medium moves from rest. Bigger amplitude = more energy.
- **Wavelength (λ)** — the distance between two matching points (crest to crest).

The Greek letter **λ** ("lambda") is the universal symbol for wavelength.

</Step>

<Step title="Stretch and grow it" kind="interactive">

Use the sliders. **Amplitude** makes the wave taller (more energetic); **wavelength** stretches each cycle out across space.

<WaveLab />

Amplitude and wavelength are independent — you can have a tall short-wavelength wave or a flat long-wavelength one.

</Step>

<Step title="What the measurements mean" kind="explain">

These numbers map onto things you can sense:

- For **light**, amplitude is brightness and wavelength is colour.
- For **sound**, amplitude is loudness and wavelength (via frequency) is pitch.

So the same handful of wave properties describe ripples, music, and rainbows alike.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- A **wave** carries **energy** through a medium without carrying the **matter** along.
- **Transverse** waves wobble across the travel direction; **longitudinal** waves wobble along it.
- **Amplitude** = size of the wobble (energy); **wavelength λ** = distance per cycle.

</Step>
