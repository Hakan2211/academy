<Step title="Tying the three together" kind="explain">

You now have the three central quantities of any circuit:

- **Voltage (V)** — the push, in volts.
- **Current (I)** — the flow, in amps.
- **Resistance (R)** — the opposition, in ohms.

They aren't independent. In 1827, Georg Ohm pinned down the exact relationship between them — a single equation that lets you find any one from the other two. It's the workhorse formula of all of electronics.

<Formula tex="V = I \times R" block />

</Step>

<Step title="The law in action" kind="interactive">

The clearest way to read Ohm's law is rearranged as **I = V / R** — current equals voltage divided by resistance. Drive the two sliders and watch the current obey.

<CircuitLab />

Double the voltage and the current doubles. Double the resistance and the current halves. The current readout is always just `V ÷ R` — that's Ohm's law computing itself in real time.

</Step>

<Step title="Rearranging the equation" kind="explain">

Because it's a simple product, you can solve for whichever quantity you need:

- Find voltage: **V = I × R**
- Find current: **I = V / R**
- Find resistance: **R = V / I**

*Worked example:* a 12 V battery drives a 4 Ω heater. The current is `I = V / R = 12 / 4 = 3 A`. Want to halve that current to 1.5 A at the same voltage? You'd need to double the resistance to 8 Ω. The whole of basic circuit design is juggling these three numbers.

</Step>

<Step title="When the law holds — and when it doesn't" kind="explain">

Ohm's law describes **ohmic** components — ones whose resistance stays constant, so current rises in a perfectly straight line with voltage. A plain resistor at steady temperature is the classic example.

<Callout type="warn" title="Not everything is ohmic">
Some components break the straight line. A **filament bulb** heats up as current rises, increasing its resistance, so it bends away. A **diode** blocks current one way entirely and lets it through the other. For these, `V = IR` still defines resistance *at each instant*, but R is no longer a fixed number.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Ohm's law** links the three core quantities: `V = I × R`.
- Rearranged: **I = V/R** and **R = V/I** — find any one from the other two.
- **Ohmic** components (resistors) keep R constant, giving current proportional to voltage.
- **Non-ohmic** ones (filament bulbs, diodes) have a resistance that changes with conditions.
