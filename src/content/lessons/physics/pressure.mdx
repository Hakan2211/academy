<Lesson>

<Step title="Force, spread out" kind="explain">

Press your palm flat against a table as hard as you can — nothing happens. Press a drawing pin against it with far *less* total force and it bites straight in. Same hand, wildly different result. The missing idea is **pressure**.

Pressure is how much force is concentrated onto each bit of area:

<Formula tex="P = \dfrac{F}{A}" block />

The *same* force makes a big difference depending on whether it's spread over a wide area or focused onto a tiny one.

</Step>

<Step title="Sharp versus blunt" kind="interactive">

Keep the force fixed and just change the contact area. Watch the pressure — and how deep the block digs in — respond.

<PressureLab />

Shrink the contact patch and the pressure rockets up, even though the weight never changed. That's the entire secret of anything sharp: a knife, a needle, a nail, an ice skate's blade. They don't push *harder* — they just funnel the push onto a sliver of area.

</Step>

<Step title="Measuring pressure" kind="explain">

The proper unit of pressure is the **pascal (Pa)**: one newton spread over one square metre. A pascal is tiny, so we usually talk in **kilopascals (kPa)**.

- The air around you presses at about **101 kPa** — roughly the weight of a small car on every square metre, which you'll meet next lesson.
- A typical car tyre is inflated to around **220 kPa**.

<Formula tex="1\ \text{Pa} = 1\ \dfrac{\text{N}}{\text{m}^2}" block />

</Step>

<Step title="Pressure all around you" kind="explain">

Once you see pressure as *force ÷ area*, everyday life is full of it — in both directions:

- **Concentrate it** to cut or pierce: knives, needles, nails, crampons, a woodpecker's beak.
- **Spread it out** to avoid sinking or damaging: snowshoes, skis, a camel's wide feet, a tractor's fat tyres, lying flat to cross thin ice.

<Callout type="tip" title="Same trick, both ways">
Want to push through something? Reduce the area. Want to avoid pushing through? Increase it. The force can stay exactly the same.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Pressure = force ÷ area** (`P = F/A`), measured in **pascals**.
- The same force gives **high pressure on a small area**, **low pressure on a large area**.
- Sharp things work by concentrating force onto a tiny area; wide things work by spreading it out.

<Formula tex="P = \dfrac{F}{A}" block />

</Step>
