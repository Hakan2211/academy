<Lesson>

<Step title="Shaped glass that steers light" kind="explain">

A **lens** is a piece of glass curved so that refraction bends rays in a controlled, useful way. There are two basic shapes:

- **Convex** (fat in the middle) — **converging**: it brings rays together.
- **Concave** (thin in the middle) — **diverging**: it spreads rays apart.

Each face refracts the light, and the curve makes every ray bend by just the right amount.

</Step>

<Step title="Rays to a focus" kind="interactive">

Send in parallel rays (as if from a distant object) and watch where they go. Toggle the lens type and drag the focal length.

<LensRays />

A converging lens funnels parallel rays to a single **focal point F**. A diverging lens fans them out as though they sprang from an F on the near side.

</Step>

<Step title="Focal length" kind="explain">

The **focal length (f)** is the distance from the lens to the focal point. A fatter, more strongly curved lens has a **shorter** focal length — it bends light more sharply.

Where parallel rays meet, you can place a screen and catch a sharp **image**. That's the principle behind every camera, projector, and telescope.

</Step>

<Step title="Lenses everywhere — including your eye" kind="explain">

You're using lenses right now. The lens in each **eye** focuses light onto the retina; muscles squeeze it to refocus from far to near.

- **Glasses & contacts** add a corrective lens when your eye focuses too short or too long.
- A **magnifying glass** (convex) makes things look bigger.
- **Cameras, microscopes, and telescopes** stack lenses to gather and focus light with precision.

<Callout type="info" title="One idea, endless uses">
Every one of these devices is just refraction, harnessed by a carefully ground curve of glass.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Convex** lenses **converge** rays; **concave** lenses **diverge** them.
- Parallel rays meet at the **focal point**; the lens-to-focus distance is the **focal length f**.
- A shorter focal length means stronger bending.
- The same physics runs your **eye**, glasses, cameras, and telescopes.

</Step>
