<Lesson>

<Step title="The ability to cause change" kind="explain">

Switch on a kettle and water heats. Kick a ball and it flies. Eat breakfast and you can run. Different stories — but each one needs the same hidden ingredient: **energy**.

Energy is the universe's way of keeping score of **change**. Anything that gets hotter, moves faster, climbs higher, or lights up is spending energy to do it. Nothing happens without it.

</Step>

<Step title="One currency, many forms" kind="explain">

Energy comes in many disguises:

- **Kinetic** — energy of motion (a rolling ball, the wind)
- **Potential** — stored energy waiting to be released (a stretched spring, water behind a dam)
- **Thermal** — the jiggling of particles we feel as heat
- **Chemical** — locked in food, fuel, and batteries
- **Electrical**, **light**, **sound**, and more

The big idea: these are all the **same currency**. Energy is constantly being swapped from one form into another.

</Step>

<Step title="Measured in joules" kind="explain">

Energy has a unit: the **joule (J)**. One joule is roughly the energy it takes to lift a small apple one metre.

<Formula tex="1\ \text{J} = 1\ \text{N} \cdot \text{m}" block />

For scale: a beating heart uses about 1 J per beat, a slice of bread holds around 300,000 J of chemical energy, and a lightning bolt unleashes billions.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Energy** is what every change costs — heating, moving, lifting, lighting.
- It comes in many **forms** (kinetic, potential, thermal, chemical…) but they're one shared currency.
- Energy constantly **converts** between forms; it's never used up, just transformed.
- We measure it in **joules (J)**.

</Step>
