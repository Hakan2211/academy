<Step title="The great accounting rule" kind="explain">

Here's one of the deepest laws in all of physics, and it's wonderfully simple:

> **Energy can't be created or destroyed — only changed from one form to another.**

Add up every kind of energy in a closed system and the total never changes. It's the universe's unbreakable accounting rule, and no experiment has ever caught it being violated.

</Step>

<Step title="Follow the energy" kind="interactive">

With friction set to zero, the ball swings forever — because its **total** energy (the dashed line) never drops. Kinetic and potential just pass the same fixed amount back and forth.

<EnergySkater />

Try to find a moment where the dashed total dips. You can't: every joule that leaves the green bar arrives in the blue one.

</Step>

<Step title="A worked example" kind="explain">

Drop a 2 kg rock from a height of 5 m. At the top it's all potential:

<Formula tex="E_p = m g h = 2 \times 9.8 \times 5 \approx 98\ \text{J}" block />

Just before it lands, height is zero, so all 98 J has become kinetic. Set them equal and solve for the speed:

<Formula tex="\tfrac{1}{2} m v^2 = 98 \implies v \approx 9.9\ \text{m/s}" block />

We never tracked the messy details of the fall — conservation let us jump straight from start to finish.

</Step>

<Step title="So why do things stop?" kind="explain">

If energy is conserved, why does a real pendulum eventually halt?

It doesn't lose energy — it **transfers** it. Friction and air resistance quietly convert the motion into **heat** and a little **sound**. Count those too and the books still balance perfectly. The energy didn't vanish; it just scattered into forms we don't usually notice.

<Callout type="tip" title="Key distinction">
*Mechanical* energy (kinetic + potential) can shrink. *Total* energy — including heat and sound — never does.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Energy is conserved**: the total in a closed system is constant.
- In a frictionless swap, **mechanical energy (KE + PE)** stays fixed.
- When things slow down, energy isn't lost — it's **transferred** into heat and sound.
- Conservation is a shortcut: compare the start and end without tracking every step in between.

</Step>
