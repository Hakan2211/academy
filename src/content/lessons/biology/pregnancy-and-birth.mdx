<Lesson>

<Step title="From zygote to baby" kind="explain">

Once an egg is fertilised, an extraordinary nine-month journey begins. That single cell — the **zygote** — divides again and again, its cells specialise, and a whole human body takes shape inside the uterus.

This is **development**, and it turns one cell into the trillions of a newborn.

</Step>

<Step title="The stages of development" kind="interactive">

Step through the journey from one cell to birth.

<Development />

The zygote divides into a ball of cells that **implants** in the uterus lining. From weeks 3–8 it's an **embryo**, where organs first form; from week 9 it's a **fetus**, mostly growing and maturing, until birth at around 40 weeks.

</Step>

<Step title="Kept alive by the placenta" kind="explain">

A developing baby can't eat or breathe for itself, so it relies on a remarkable temporary organ — the **placenta** — connected by the **umbilical cord**.

At the placenta, the mother's blood and the baby's blood flow close together (but never mix). Across this barrier, by **diffusion**:

- **Oxygen and nutrients** pass from mother to baby.
- **Carbon dioxide and waste** pass from baby to mother.

The fluid-filled **amniotic sac** cushions and protects the baby throughout.

At the end, **birth** happens: the muscular wall of the uterus contracts powerfully (labour), the cervix opens, and the baby is pushed out into the world to take its first breath.

<Callout type="tip" title="The placenta as a gateway">
The placenta is a lifeline — but it's also a route for harmful things. Alcohol, nicotine, and some drugs and infections can cross it to the baby, which is why a healthy pregnancy means protecting that connection.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- A fertilised egg develops over ~9 months: **zygote → ball of cells → embryo** (organs form) **→ fetus** (grows) **→ birth**.
- The **placenta** (via the umbilical cord) exchanges **oxygen/nutrients** and **waste** by diffusion between mother and baby — their blood never mixes.
- The **amniotic sac** protects the baby; **birth** comes when the uterus contracts and the cervix opens.

</Step>
