<Step title="Fats, oils, and more" kind="explain">

The **lipids** are life's fats and oils. Like carbohydrates they're built from carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen — but with far **less oxygen**, which is why they're greasy, don't mix with water, and pack in a lot of energy.

Gram for gram, fat stores roughly **twice** the energy of carbohydrate — making it the body's dense, long-term energy reserve.

</Step>

A typical fat — a **triglyceride** — is one **glycerol** molecule with three **fatty acid** tails attached. Those long hydrocarbon tails are where the energy is stored.

</Step>

<Step title="More than energy storage" kind="explain">

Lipids do several vital jobs beyond fuel:

- **Energy store** — dense, long-term reserves under your skin and around organs.
- **Insulation & protection** — body fat keeps you warm and cushions organs.
- **Membranes** — a special lipid, the **phospholipid**, builds every cell membrane (remember the fluid mosaic). Its water-loving head and water-hating tails are what make the bilayer form.
- **Steroids** — lipids include hormones like testosterone and oestrogen.

<Callout type="tip" title="Full circle">
The phospholipid bilayer you met in the membrane world is a lipid. Without lipids that refuse to mix with water, there could be no membrane — and no cells.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Lipids** (C, H, O — little O) are fats and oils: water-insoluble and energy-dense.
- A fat is a **triglyceride** — glycerol + three **fatty acids**.
- Jobs: dense **energy storage**, **insulation/protection**, **steroid** hormones, and — crucially — the **phospholipids** that build every membrane.

</Step>
