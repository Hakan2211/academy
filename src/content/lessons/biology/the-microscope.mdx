<Lesson>

<Step title="Below the limit of the eye" kind="explain">

Hold up your hand. The smallest thing your unaided eye can pick out is about **0.1 mm** across — roughly the width of a fine hair. A typical cell is *ten times smaller* than that.

For most of history, an entire universe of life was simply invisible. Then, in the 1600s, the microscope cracked it open — and biology discovered the **cell**, the building block of everything alive.

A microscope does two jobs, and it's vital to keep them apart: **magnification** and **resolution**.

</Step>

<Step title="Turn up the magnification" kind="interactive">

Drag the magnification slider. Watch a flat green smudge resolve into a patchwork of plant **cells**, then into the structures *inside* them.

<MicroscopeZoom />

**Magnification** is how much bigger the image looks. But notice two things as you zoom: you see more detail — and your **field of view** shrinks. Crank it high enough and you're staring at a single cell.

</Step>

<Step title="Magnification vs resolution" kind="explain">

Magnification alone is useless without **resolution** — the ability to tell two close points apart. Blow up a blurry photo and you just get a bigger blur; no new detail appears.

- A **light microscope** resolves down to about 200 nm — good enough for cells and large organelles like the nucleus.
- An **electron microscope** uses beams of electrons instead of light and resolves below 1 nm — sharp enough to see individual viruses and the machinery inside a cell.

<Callout type="info" title="Why electrons win">
Light can't resolve anything much smaller than its own wavelength. Electrons behave like waves thousands of times shorter, so they reveal a world light can never reach.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- The naked eye stops at about 0.1 mm; cells are far smaller, so we need microscopes.
- **Magnification** = how much bigger; **resolution** = how much detail. Both matter.
- Light microscopes reveal cells and big organelles; **electron microscopes** reveal viruses and fine internal structure.
- The microscope is the instrument that revealed the cell — and launched modern biology.

</Step>
