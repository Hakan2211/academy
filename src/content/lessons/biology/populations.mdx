<Step title="What sets a population's size?" kind="explain">

A **population** is all the organisms of one species in an area. Its size is never fixed — it grows, shrinks, and fluctuates as conditions change. Understanding what controls it is central to ecology and conservation.

Two forces push against each other: the drive of organisms to **reproduce** (which would make numbers explode) and the limits of the **environment** (which hold them in check).

</Step>

<Step title="Growth and its limits" kind="interactive">

Switch between the two patterns.

<PopulationGraph />

- **Logistic growth:** a population grows fast at first, then slows and levels off at the **carrying capacity** — the maximum the habitat can sustain, set by food, space, water, and predators.
- **Predator–prey cycles:** predator and prey numbers chase each other up and down, the predator peak always lagging behind the prey.

</Step>

<Step title="Competition and limiting factors" kind="explain">

A population can't grow forever because resources run short. The things that hold it back are **limiting factors**:

- **Competition** for food, water, space, light, or mates — both *within* a species and *between* species.
- **Predation** — predators keep prey numbers down.
- **Disease** — spreads faster when a population is crowded.

When two species need the same limited resource, they **compete**, and the better-adapted one tends to win (a link straight back to natural selection). This is why no population grows unchecked — there's always a ceiling set by the environment.

<Callout type="tip" title="Predator–prey, explained">
More rabbits → foxes have plenty to eat and multiply → more foxes eat the rabbits down → rabbits become scarce → foxes starve and decline → with fewer foxes, rabbits recover. And the cycle repeats.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- A **population** is all of one species in an area; its size rises and falls.
- **Logistic growth** levels off at the **carrying capacity** (the most the habitat supports).
- **Predator and prey** numbers cycle, each chasing the other.
- Growth is held in check by **limiting factors**: **competition**, **predation**, and **disease**.

</Step>
