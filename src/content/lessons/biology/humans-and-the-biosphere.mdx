<Lesson>

<Step title="One species, a planetary force" kind="explain">

Every species affects its environment, but one has come to reshape the **entire planet**: us. There are now over **8 billion** humans, and our farming, cities, industry, and waste touch every ecosystem on Earth — the whole **biosphere**.

Understanding our impact is one of the most important applications of biology, because the same science that explains the damage also points to the solutions.

</Step>

<Step title="Tipping the balance" kind="interactive">

Take the carbon cycle, which kept atmospheric CO₂ steady for millennia. Now switch on human emissions.

<CarbonCycle />

By **burning fossil fuels** — coal, oil, and gas, which locked away carbon over millions of years — we release CO₂ far faster than photosynthesis can reabsorb it. The natural balance is broken, and CO₂ builds up in the atmosphere.

</Step>

<Step title="Our footprint" kind="explain">

Human pressure on the biosphere takes several forms:

- **Pollution** — of air (smog, CO₂), water (sewage, fertilisers, plastic), and land (waste, chemicals).
- **Land use** — deforestation and farming destroy habitats and reduce biodiversity.
- **Overexploitation** — overfishing and overhunting outpace species' recovery.
- **Climate change** — the build-up of greenhouse gases is warming the planet (the next, final deep-dive).

These problems are connected: fertiliser run-off can cause **eutrophication** (algae bloom, then oxygen crashes, killing fish); deforestation both destroys habitat *and* removes the trees that absorb CO₂.

<Callout type="tip" title="The science cuts both ways">
The biology that reveals these problems also shows the way out: protecting forests and oceans (which absorb carbon), switching to clean energy, reducing waste, and conserving biodiversity. Understanding the systems is the first step to repairing them.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- Humans have become a **planetary force**, reshaping the whole **biosphere**.
- Burning **fossil fuels** unbalances the carbon cycle, raising atmospheric CO₂.
- Our footprint includes **pollution, habitat destruction, overexploitation,** and **climate change** — often interconnected (e.g. eutrophication).
- The same biology reveals the **solutions**: clean energy, conservation, and protecting carbon-absorbing ecosystems.

</Step>
