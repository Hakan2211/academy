<Step title="The variety of life" kind="explain">

**Biodiversity** is the variety of living things — the number of different species in an ecosystem, on a continent, or across the whole planet. From bacteria to blue whales, Earth is home to millions of species, and that richness is precious.

It's also the product of everything in this subject: billions of years of **evolution** filling every available way of life.

</Step>

<Step title="Why biodiversity matters" kind="interactive">

A diverse ecosystem is a **resilient** one. Recall the food web — and try removing a species again.

<FoodWeb />

In a web with lots of species, losing one is cushioned: predators can switch to other prey. But in a simple ecosystem with few species, losing one can cause a **collapse**. High biodiversity is an ecosystem's insurance against disaster.

</Step>

<Step title="Threats and conservation" kind="explain">

Biodiversity is falling fast, and human activity is the main cause:

- **Habitat destruction** — clearing forests and draining wetlands for farms and cities.
- **Pollution** and **climate change** — making habitats unliveable.
- **Overhunting and overfishing** — taking species faster than they can recover.
- **Invasive species** — introduced organisms that outcompete the natives.

We protect it through **conservation**: nature reserves and protected areas, breeding endangered species, restoring habitats, and protecting whole ecosystems. The motivation is both practical and ethical — biodiversity gives us food, medicines, clean air and water, and stable ecosystems, on top of its own intrinsic worth.

<Callout type="info" title="Why a beetle matters">
Every species is a thread in the web. Some are keystone species holding whole ecosystems together; others may hold an undiscovered medicine. Losing biodiversity weakens the systems all life — including us — depends on.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Biodiversity** is the variety of species — the product of evolution.
- It makes ecosystems **resilient**; low-diversity systems collapse more easily.
- It's threatened by **habitat loss, pollution, climate change, overexploitation,** and **invasive species**.
- **Conservation** (reserves, breeding programmes, habitat restoration) protects it — for practical, ecological, and ethical reasons.

</Step>
