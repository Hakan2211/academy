<Step title="Beyond bacteria and viruses" kind="explain">

The microbial world isn't only bacteria and viruses. Two more groups round it out — and unlike bacteria, both are **eukaryotes** (their cells have a nucleus). They're the **fungi** and the **protists**.

They're easy to overlook, but they shape ecosystems, our food, our medicine — and some cause serious disease.

</Step>

<Step title="Two more kingdoms" kind="interactive">

**Fungi** feed by absorbing nutrients and are the world's great **decomposers**. **Protists** are a grab-bag of mostly single-celled eukaryotes — some, like algae, photosynthesise and produce much of our oxygen.

</Step>

<Step title="Why they matter" kind="explain">

Both groups are double-edged — hugely beneficial, occasionally deadly:

- **Fungi** recycle dead matter (returning nutrients to soil), give us bread and beer (yeast), and gave us the first antibiotic, **penicillin** (from a mould). But some cause disease, like athlete's foot and ringworm, and moulds can spoil crops.
- **Protists** include the algae and plankton that form the base of ocean food chains and make much of Earth's oxygen. But one protist, **Plasmodium**, causes **malaria** — among the deadliest diseases in human history, spread by mosquitoes.

So the microbial world is far more than germs: it's the recycling system, the oxygen factory, and the food base of the planet — with a dangerous minority.

<Callout type="info" title="Pathogens come in all four types">
Disease can be caused by **bacteria** (tuberculosis), **viruses** (flu), **fungi** (athlete's foot), or **protists** (malaria). Knowing which kind a pathogen is matters, because each needs different treatment.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Fungi** and **protists** are **eukaryotic** microbes.
- **Fungi**: decomposers; give us bread, beer, and penicillin; some cause disease.
- **Protists**: mostly single-celled; algae/plankton make oxygen and feed food chains; **Plasmodium** causes malaria.
- Pathogens can be **bacteria, viruses, fungi, or protists** — each needing different treatment.

</Step>
