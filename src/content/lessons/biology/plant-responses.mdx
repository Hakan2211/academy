<Step title="Plants do respond" kind="explain">

Plants have no nerves, no muscles, no brain — yet they clearly **respond** to their surroundings. A houseplant leans toward the window; a seedling pushes its shoot up and its roots down, whichever way you plant the seed.

Plants respond by **growing** in a particular direction. A growth response toward or away from a stimulus is called a **tropism**, and it's controlled by **hormones**.

</Step>

<Step title="Growing toward the light" kind="interactive">

Move the light and watch the shoot follow.

<PlantTropism />

This is **phototropism** — growth toward light. It's driven by a plant hormone called **auxin**, which gathers on the **shaded** side of the shoot, making those cells grow longer. With one side growing faster, the shoot **bends toward the light**.

</Step>

<Step title="Light, gravity, and why it helps" kind="explain">

Plants sense two main directional cues:

- **Phototropism** — shoots grow **toward light** (to maximise photosynthesis).
- **Gravitropism** — roots grow **down**, with gravity (to find water and anchor); shoots grow **up**, against it.

Both are coordinated by **auxin** redistributing to one side of the growing tip. The benefit is clear: a plant that grows its leaves into the light and its roots into the soil is far better placed to survive.

<Callout type="tip" title="Auxin in the garden">
Because auxin controls growth, artificial versions are used as **weedkillers** (making weeds grow uncontrollably) and as **rooting powders** (to make cuttings grow roots) — biology put to practical use.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- Plants respond to their environment by **growing** — a directional growth response is a **tropism**.
- **Phototropism**: shoots grow toward **light**. **Gravitropism**: roots grow **down**, shoots grow **up**.
- These are controlled by the hormone **auxin**, which gathers on one side of the growing tip and makes it grow longer.
- Auxin is used commercially as weedkiller and rooting powder.

</Step>
