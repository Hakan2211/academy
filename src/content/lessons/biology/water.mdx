<Step title="The molecule life is built around" kind="explain">

You are about **60% water**. Every reaction in every cell happens in it; blood, sap, and cytoplasm are mostly it. Life began in it and can't survive long without it.

Why does one small, simple molecule matter so much? Because of a subtle trick of its shape: water is **lopsided**, and that makes it behave in ways that turn out to be perfect for life.

</Step>

<Step title="A lopsided molecule" kind="interactive">

Explore water's three life-giving properties.

<WaterMolecule />

It comes down to **polarity**: oxygen pulls the shared electrons toward itself, so the oxygen end is slightly negative and the hydrogen ends slightly positive. Those gentle charges let water molecules cling to each other and to other substances.

</Step>

<Step title="Why it matters for life" kind="explain">

Water's polarity gives it a set of life-supporting talents:

- **A great solvent** — it surrounds and dissolves other charged molecules, so reactions can happen and substances can be transported (in blood, in sap).
- **Cohesion** — water molecules stick together by hydrogen bonds, creating surface tension and letting columns of water be pulled up tall trees.
- **Temperature stability** — it takes a lot of heat to warm water, so it buffers cells and whole oceans against sudden temperature swings.

<Callout type="tip" title="The transport medium of life">
Because water dissolves so much, it's biology's delivery system. Nutrients, gases, and wastes all travel dissolved in water — in your blood, your cells, and the sap of plants.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- Water makes up most of every organism and is where life's chemistry happens.
- It is **polar** — lopsided charge — which drives all its useful properties.
- That makes it an excellent **solvent**, gives it **cohesion** (surface tension, water rising in plants), and lets it **buffer temperature**.

</Step>
