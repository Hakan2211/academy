<Step title="Built to make and meet gametes" kind="explain">

Humans reproduce sexually, which means combining two **gametes** (sex cells): a **sperm** from the male and an **egg** (ovum) from the female. The male and female reproductive systems are each built for one job — to **make** gametes and bring them together so fertilisation can happen.

</Step>

<Step title="The organs and their jobs" kind="interactive">

Toggle between female and male, and click each part.

<ReproductiveSystem />

In the **female**, the **ovaries** make and release eggs, which travel down the **oviducts** to the **uterus**. In the **male**, the **testes** make sperm continuously, which travel via the **sperm duct** and are delivered through the **penis**.

</Step>

<Step title="Making the gametes" kind="explain">

Gametes are special: they're made by **meiosis**, so they carry only **23** chromosomes (half the normal 46). When sperm and egg join at fertilisation, the full 46 is restored — and the child gets a mix of both parents' genes.

The two systems differ in tempo:

- **Males** make sperm **continuously** from puberty onward — millions every day.
- **Females** are born with all their eggs already present, and release roughly **one per month** from puberty.

This monthly release of an egg is governed by a hormonal rhythm — the menstrual cycle — coming up shortly.

<Callout type="info" title="Adapted gametes">
A sperm is tiny, with a tail to swim and lots of mitochondria for energy — built to travel. An egg is large, packed with food reserves to nourish the early embryo — built to stay and grow.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- Human sexual reproduction joins two **gametes**: a **sperm** (male) and an **egg** (female).
- **Female:** ovaries make/release eggs → oviducts → uterus. **Male:** testes make sperm → sperm duct → penis.
- Gametes are made by **meiosis**, carrying **23** chromosomes, so fertilisation restores **46**.
- Males make sperm continuously; females release ~one egg a month.

</Step>
