<Lesson>

<Step title="Alive or not?" kind="explain">

A **virus** is the strangest thing in biology. It's far smaller than a bacterium — just a bit of genetic material (DNA or RNA) wrapped in a protein coat. It has no cells, no cytoplasm, no way to make energy, and **cannot reproduce on its own**.

On its own, a virus does absolutely nothing — it's as inert as a crystal. That's why most biologists say a virus **isn't truly alive**. It only springs to life by hijacking a living cell.

</Step>

<Step title="Hijacking a cell" kind="interactive">

Step through how a virus reproduces — the **lytic cycle**.

<VirusReplication />

The virus attaches to a host cell, injects its genes, and **takes over the cell's machinery**, forcing it to build hundreds of new viruses. Then the cell bursts, releasing them to infect more cells.

</Step>

<Step title="Why viruses are hard to fight" kind="explain">

Because a virus hides and replicates *inside* your own cells, it's a tricky enemy:

- **Antibiotics don't work on viruses** — antibiotics target features of bacterial cells, which viruses don't have. Taking them for a cold or flu is useless (and fuels resistance).
- Viruses **mutate fast**, especially RNA viruses, so they can dodge the immune system — which is why the **flu vaccine** is remade every year.

Viruses cause many familiar diseases: colds, flu, measles, chickenpox, HIV/AIDS, and COVID-19. The main defences are your **immune system** and **vaccines** — both coming up shortly.

<Callout type="tip" title="The big mistake to avoid">
Antibiotics kill bacteria, not viruses. A sore throat or cold is usually viral, so antibiotics won't help — and using them needlessly speeds the rise of resistant bacteria.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- A **virus** is genetic material in a protein coat — not made of cells and probably **not alive**.
- It can only reproduce by **hijacking a host cell** (the lytic cycle): attach → inject → replicate → assemble → burst.
- **Antibiotics don't work** on viruses; they mutate fast (so the flu vaccine changes yearly).
- Defences are the **immune system** and **vaccines**.

</Step>
