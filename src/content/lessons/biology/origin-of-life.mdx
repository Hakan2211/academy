<Lesson>

<Step title="The hardest question" kind="explain">

Evolution explains how life **diversified** from a common ancestor. But it leaves a deeper question: where did that first living thing come from? How did **non-living chemistry** become the first cell?

This is the **origin of life** — one of science's great open problems. We don't have a complete answer, but we have a plausible, evidence-backed story, and we can test its steps in the lab.

</Step>

<Step title="From chemistry to cell" kind="interactive">

Step through one widely-studied scenario for how it could have happened.

<OriginTimeline />

The crucial leap is between stages: from simple molecules, to molecules that can **copy themselves**, to molecules wrapped in a **membrane** — which is essentially a primitive cell.

</Step>

<Step title="The key ideas" kind="explain">

A few pieces make the story credible:

- **Building blocks form easily.** The famous Miller–Urey experiment showed that sparking the early atmosphere's gases produces **amino acids** — life's building blocks — spontaneously.
- **RNA could do both jobs.** Modern life splits labour between DNA (information) and proteins (catalysis). But **RNA can do both** — store information *and* catalyse reactions — so an early "**RNA world**" could have got self-copying going before DNA or proteins existed.
- **Membranes self-assemble.** Lipid molecules naturally form bubbles in water, providing a ready-made "container" to separate an inside from an outside.

None of this needs a miracle — just chemistry, energy, and a very long time.

<Callout type="tip" title="Origin ≠ evolution">
Be careful to separate the two. **Evolution** explains how life changed once it existed — and is overwhelmingly supported. The **origin** of that first life is a separate, harder, still-open question.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- The **origin of life** asks how non-living chemistry became the first cell — a separate question from evolution.
- A plausible path: simple gases → **organic building blocks** (Miller–Urey) → **self-copying RNA** (the RNA world) → **membrane-bound protocells** → first true cells.
- It requires only chemistry, energy, and deep time — but remains an **open** scientific question.

</Step>
