<Lesson>

<Step title="Life in communities" kind="explain">

No organism lives alone. An **ecosystem** is all the living things in an area together with their non-living surroundings — a pond, a forest, a coral reef — all interacting. **Ecology** is the study of these interactions.

And the thing that powers every ecosystem, almost without exception, is **sunlight** — captured by plants and passed along from one organism to the next.

</Step>

<Step title="The pyramid of energy" kind="interactive">

Energy enters through **producers** (plants) and flows up through the **consumers** that eat them. But click through the levels and notice something striking.

<EnergyPyramid />

At each step, only about **10%** of the energy passes on. The other 90% is lost — used for movement, lost as heat from respiration, or passed out as waste. Energy doesn't cycle; it flows **one way** and dwindles.

</Step>

<Step title="Why food chains are short" kind="explain">

That 10% rule has big consequences for how ecosystems are shaped:

- **Food chains are short** — usually 3 to 5 links. After a few steps, there's simply too little energy left to support another level.
- **Top predators are rare** — there's only enough energy up there for a few of them.
- **Producers are abundant** — the wide base of the pyramid feeds everything above.

This is also why eating plants (lower down the chain) feeds far more people than eating meat: every step up wastes 90% of the energy.

<Callout type="info" title="Energy flows, matter cycles">
Here's the key distinction for the whole world: **energy** flows one way (in from the Sun, out as heat) and is never recycled. **Matter** (carbon, nitrogen, water) is recycled round and round. We'll meet the matter cycles soon.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- An **ecosystem** is the living things in an area plus their non-living environment.
- Energy enters via **producers** (photosynthesis) and flows up through **consumers**.
- Only ~**10%** passes to each level (the rest is lost), so **food chains are short** and **top predators are rare**.
- **Energy flows one way; matter cycles.**

</Step>
