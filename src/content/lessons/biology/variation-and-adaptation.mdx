<Lesson>

<Step title="No two alike" kind="explain">

Look at any group of living things of the same kind — a field of poppies, a crowd of people, a colony of beetles — and you'll see they're all slightly **different**. Taller, darker, faster, more resistant. This **variation** is everywhere, and it's the starting point for everything in this world.

Variation comes from two sources: **mutations** (new alleles) and the **shuffling** of meiosis and sexual reproduction. Without it, evolution would have nothing to work with.

</Step>

<Step title="Variation you can act on" kind="interactive">

Here's a population that varies in one trait — colour. Notice some individuals blend into the background better than others.

<NaturalSelection preset="beetles" />

That variation isn't just decoration. In a given environment, some variants survive a little better than others. A feature that helps an organism survive is called an **adaptation**.

</Step>

<Step title="Adaptations fit the environment" kind="explain">

An **adaptation** is any inherited feature that helps an organism survive and reproduce in its surroundings:

- A polar bear's white fur and thick fat — camouflage and insulation for the Arctic.
- A cactus's spines and water-storing stem — survival in the desert.
- A moth's bark-coloured wings — hiding from birds.

The key word is **inherited**. A suntan helps you in the sun, but you can't pass it on — so it isn't an adaptation in the evolutionary sense. Adaptations are written in the genes.

<Callout type="info" title="Variation is the raw material">
Evolution can only select from what's already there. The more varied a population, the more raw material natural selection has to work with — which is why variation matters so much.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Variation** — differences between individuals of the same kind — is everywhere, from **mutation** and **sexual reproduction**.
- An **adaptation** is an **inherited** feature that helps an organism survive and reproduce in its environment.
- Variation is the **raw material** that natural selection acts on.

</Step>
