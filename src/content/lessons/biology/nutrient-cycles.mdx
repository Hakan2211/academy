<Lesson>

<Step title="Matter goes round" kind="explain">

Energy flows through an ecosystem one way and is lost. But the **atoms** — carbon, nitrogen, oxygen, water — are used over and over, cycling endlessly between living things and the environment. The carbon in your body has been part of countless other organisms before you, and will be again.

These loops are the **nutrient cycles**, and the unsung heroes that keep them turning are the **decomposers**.

</Step>

<Step title="The carbon cycle" kind="interactive">

The most important is the **carbon cycle**. Watch carbon move between the air, plants, animals, and soil.

<CarbonCycle />

- **Photosynthesis** pulls CO₂ *out* of the air into plants.
- **Respiration** (by plants, animals, and decomposers) puts it *back*.
- **Feeding** passes carbon along food chains; **death and decomposition** return it to the soil and air.

For most of history these have balanced, keeping atmospheric CO₂ roughly steady.

</Step>

<Step title="Nitrogen, water, and the decomposers" kind="explain">

Carbon isn't the only element on a loop:

- **The nitrogen cycle:** the air is 78% nitrogen, but life can't use it directly. Special **bacteria** "fix" it into forms plants can absorb; animals get it by eating plants; decomposers and other bacteria return it to the air. Nitrogen is needed for **proteins and DNA**.
- **The water cycle:** water evaporates, forms clouds, falls as rain, is taken up by organisms, and returns — driven by the Sun.

In every cycle, **decomposers** (bacteria and fungi) do the vital job of breaking down dead organisms and waste, releasing the locked-up nutrients back for living things to reuse.

<Callout type="info" title="Without decomposers...">
Dead bodies and waste would pile up, and the nutrients locked inside them would never return to the soil. Plants would run out of nutrients, and life would grind to a halt. Decomposers are the recyclers that keep everything going.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Matter cycles** (unlike energy): atoms are reused endlessly between organisms and the environment.
- The **carbon cycle**: photosynthesis removes CO₂; respiration, feeding, and decomposition return it.
- The **nitrogen cycle** relies on **bacteria** to make air nitrogen usable (for proteins/DNA); the **water cycle** is Sun-driven.
- **Decomposers** are essential — they release locked-up nutrients back into the cycle.

</Step>
