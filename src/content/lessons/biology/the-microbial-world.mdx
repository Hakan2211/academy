<Lesson>

<Step title="The invisible majority" kind="explain">

Look around and you see plants and animals. But the most abundant life on Earth is invisible: **microbes**. There are more bacteria in a spoonful of soil than there are people on Earth, and more bacterial cells in (and on) your body than your own cells.

The simplest and oldest are the **bacteria** (and their cousins the **archaea**) — single **prokaryotic** cells with no nucleus, which we met right back in the cell world.

</Step>

A bacterium has a **cell wall**, a **membrane**, **cytoplasm** with ribosomes, and — instead of a nucleus — a single loop of **DNA** floating free, sometimes with small extra rings called **plasmids**. Many have a **flagellum** to swim.

</Step>

<Step title="Friend and foe" kind="explain">

It's tempting to think "microbe = germ", but most bacteria are harmless or downright helpful:

- **Helpful:** bacteria in your gut help digest food and make vitamins; others fix nitrogen for plants, make yoghurt and cheese, and break down waste.
- **Harmful:** a minority are **pathogens** that cause disease — like the bacteria behind food poisoning, tuberculosis, and tetanus.

Bacteria reproduce **asexually** by simply splitting in two, and they can do it incredibly fast — every 20 minutes in good conditions — which is why an infection can explode, and why they evolve so quickly.

<Callout type="info" title="Why plasmids matter">
Bacteria can swap plasmids with each other, passing genes around — including genes for **antibiotic resistance**. That gene-sharing is a big part of why resistance spreads so fast (a later lesson).
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Microbes** are the most abundant life on Earth; the oldest are **bacteria** and **archaea** — prokaryotes with no nucleus.
- A bacterium has a wall, membrane, cytoplasm, a loop of **DNA**, often **plasmids**, and a **flagellum**.
- Most bacteria are harmless or helpful; a minority are **pathogens**.
- They reproduce fast by splitting — and swap **plasmids** (spreading genes like resistance).

</Step>
