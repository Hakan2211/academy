<Lesson>

<Step title="How do we know?" kind="explain">

Evolution happens far too slowly to watch a fish become a mammal. So how can we be confident it occurred? The answer is that **many independent lines of evidence** — from rocks, bodies, embryos, and molecules — all tell the same story. When unrelated methods agree, the conclusion is hard to escape.

</Step>

<Step title="Four windows onto the past" kind="interactive">

Switch between the four kinds of evidence.

<EvidenceTabs />

Each on its own is suggestive. Together — fossils showing change over time, shared anatomy, near-identical embryos, and matching DNA — they point overwhelmingly to common descent.

</Step>

<Step title="The power of agreement" kind="explain">

Look at how they reinforce one another:

- **Fossils** show life changing through the rock layers, with transitional forms linking groups.
- **Homologous structures** — the same arm bones in a human, bat, and whale — reveal a shared ancestor, repurposed for different jobs.
- **Embryos** of fish, birds, and humans look alike early on, betraying deep common ancestry.
- **DNA** clinches it: the more recently two species diverged, the more genes they share — and the family tree built from DNA matches the one built from fossils and anatomy.

That last point is the killer. The tree of life drawn from molecules — which Darwin never saw — independently confirms the tree drawn from bones and fossils.

<Callout type="info" title="Vestigial clues">
Some structures are leftovers: the tiny hip bones inside a whale, your own appendix, the remains of a tailbone. These **vestigial** features make sense only as inheritances from ancestors that used them.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- Evolution is supported by **many independent lines of evidence** that agree.
- **Fossils** (change over time), **homologous anatomy** (shared body plans), **embryos** (shared development), and **DNA** (genetic similarity).
- The DNA-based tree of life **independently matches** the one from fossils and anatomy.
- **Vestigial** structures are leftovers from ancestors.

</Step>
