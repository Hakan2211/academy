<Step title="Life is chemistry" kind="explain">

Strip a living thing down to its molecules and you find no magic ingredient — just ordinary atoms, the same ones in rocks and air, arranged in extraordinary ways. Biology, at its base, is **chemistry that has learned to copy itself**.

A handful of elements make up almost all of life: **carbon, hydrogen, oxygen, and nitrogen**, with a little phosphorus and sulfur. And one of them — carbon — is the quiet genius behind it all.

</Step>

<Step title="Carbon: the master builder" kind="interactive">

Carbon forms exactly **four** bonds. That simple fact lets it link into chains, rings, and branches of limitless variety — the scaffolding for every biological molecule. Toggle the shapes.

<CarbonBackbone />

No other common element is so versatile. Carbon's four-bond habit is why life is "carbon-based" — it can build molecules big and intricate enough to carry the machinery of living.

</Step>

<Step title="From small to giant" kind="explain">

Life's big molecules are **polymers** — long chains assembled from small repeating units called **monomers**, like beads on a string.

- Link many small sugars → a **carbohydrate**.
- Link many amino acids → a **protein**.
- Link many nucleotides → a **nucleic acid** (DNA).

Cells build polymers by joining monomers (removing water, a reaction called **condensation**) and break them down by adding water (**hydrolysis**). The rest of this world tours the four great families these chains belong to.

<Callout type="info" title="Same trick, four families">
Carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, and nucleic acids are all built by stringing together small units. Master that one idea and the biochemistry falls into place.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- Life is built from ordinary atoms — mainly **C, H, O, N** (plus P and S).
- **Carbon** forms **four bonds**, building chains, rings, and branches — the basis of "carbon-based" life.
- Big molecules are **polymers** of small **monomers**, joined by **condensation** and split by **hydrolysis**.
- Four families dominate: carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, nucleic acids.

</Step>
