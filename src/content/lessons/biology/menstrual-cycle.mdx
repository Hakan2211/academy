<Step title="A monthly rhythm" kind="explain">

From puberty, the female body runs a roughly **28-day cycle** that prepares it for a possible pregnancy each month. It releases an egg and builds up the uterus lining to receive it — and if no pregnancy occurs, resets and starts again.

The whole cycle is orchestrated by **hormones**, a perfect example of chemical control from the previous world.

</Step>

<Step title="Tracking the cycle" kind="interactive">

Drag the day and watch the hormones rise and fall, the lining build, and ovulation happen.

<MenstrualCycle />

- **Days 1–5:** the lining breaks down — a **period** (menstruation).
- **Days 6–13:** **oestrogen** rises and rebuilds the lining.
- **Day 14:** **ovulation** — an egg is released.
- **Days 15–28:** **progesterone** maintains the lining, ready for a fertilised egg.

</Step>

<Step title="The hormonal control loop" kind="explain">

Two main hormones run the show:

- **Oestrogen** (from the ovaries) — rebuilds the uterus lining and triggers the release of the egg.
- **Progesterone** — maintains the thick lining after ovulation, ready to support a pregnancy.

If the egg **is** fertilised, progesterone stays high and the lining is kept — pregnancy begins. If it **isn't**, progesterone falls, the lining can't be maintained, and it's shed as the next period. That falling progesterone is the trigger that starts the cycle over.

<Callout type="info" title="Hormones in control of fertility">
Because hormones control the cycle, hormones can also control fertility. The contraceptive pill uses hormones to prevent ovulation; fertility treatments use them to stimulate it.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- The **menstrual cycle** (~28 days) prepares the body for pregnancy each month.
- **Days 1–5** period; **oestrogen** rebuilds the lining; **ovulation** ~day 14; **progesterone** maintains the lining.
- If no fertilisation, **progesterone falls** → the lining sheds → the cycle restarts.
- Hormones control the cycle — and so can control fertility (the pill, fertility treatment).

</Step>
