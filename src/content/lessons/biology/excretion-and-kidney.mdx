<Step title="Taking out the trash" kind="explain">

Your cells are chemical factories, and like any factory they produce **waste**. Letting it build up would poison you, so the body must constantly remove it — a process called **excretion**.

Two wastes matter most: **carbon dioxide** (from respiration, removed by the lungs) and **urea** (from breaking down excess protein, removed by the **kidneys**). The kidneys are the body's remarkable filtration plant.

</Step>

<Step title="How the kidney cleans the blood" kind="interactive">

Each kidney filters your entire blood supply many times a day. Click through the three steps.

<Nephron />

It works by being deliberately wasteful then thrifty: **filter** almost everything small out of the blood, then **reabsorb** back only what the body needs. Whatever's left becomes **urine**.

</Step>

<Step title="Filter, reclaim, balance" kind="explain">

The genius is in the two-step design:

1. **Filtration** — high pressure squeezes water, glucose, salts, and urea out of the blood (cells and proteins stay behind, too big to pass).
2. **Reabsorption** — the body then reclaims **all** the glucose and exactly the right amount of water and salt.

This does two jobs at once:

- It **removes urea** (excretion).
- It **controls water balance** — a kind of **homeostasis** (the next world's theme). Drink a lot and you make dilute urine; dehydrate and you make small amounts of concentrated urine, saving water.

<Callout type="tip" title="Why filter then reabsorb?">
It seems odd to throw glucose out only to take it back. But filtering everything small and then selectively reabsorbing is far simpler than trying to pick out just the waste — so that's how the kidney does it.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Excretion** removes the waste products of metabolism — mainly **CO₂** (lungs) and **urea** (kidneys).
- The kidney works in two steps: **filtration** (squeeze small molecules out of the blood) then **reabsorption** (reclaim glucose, water, and salts).
- What's left becomes **urine**.
- The kidney also controls the body's **water balance** — a form of homeostasis.

</Step>
