<Lesson>

<Step title="The most complex object we know" kind="explain">

Your **brain** contains around 86 **billion** neurons, wired by trillions of connections. It's the control centre of the whole nervous system — running your body, processing your senses, and producing thought, memory, and you.

It isn't one uniform lump, though. Different **regions** specialise in different jobs.

</Step>

<Step title="Mapping the brain" kind="interactive">

Click each region to see what it does.

<BrainMap />

- The **cerebrum** — thinking, memory, senses, and voluntary movement.
- The **cerebellum** — balance and coordinated movement.
- The **medulla** (brain stem) — automatic vital functions like heart rate and breathing.
- The **hypothalamus** — homeostasis and the link to hormones.

</Step>

<Step title="Feeding the brain: the senses" kind="explain">

The brain is only as good as the information it gets, and that comes from your **sense organs**, each packed with **receptors** tuned to one kind of stimulus:

- **Eyes** — light (vision).
- **Ears** — sound, and balance.
- **Skin** — touch, pressure, temperature, and pain.
- **Nose and tongue** — chemicals (smell and taste).

Each receptor turns its stimulus into nerve impulses, which travel to the brain. The brain then **interprets** them — meaning what you see and hear is really your brain's *reconstruction* of the world, built from streams of impulses.

<Callout type="info" title="How we study the brain">
Much of what we know comes from studying damage — when a stroke or injury harms one region, the lost ability reveals what that region did. Modern scanners now let us watch the living brain at work.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- The **brain** is the body's control centre, with specialised regions: **cerebrum** (thought/senses/movement), **cerebellum** (balance/coordination), **medulla** (vital automatic functions), **hypothalamus** (homeostasis).
- **Sense organs** contain **receptors** for specific stimuli (eyes—light, ears—sound, skin—touch, nose/tongue—chemicals).
- The brain **interprets** the impulses — perception is the brain's reconstruction of the world.

</Step>
