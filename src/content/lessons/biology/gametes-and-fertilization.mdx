<Lesson>

<Step title="The moment a new life begins" kind="explain">

A whole human being begins as a **single cell** — formed the instant a sperm meets an egg. This is **fertilisation**: the fusion of two gametes into one cell, the **zygote**, which carries a brand-new, unique combination of genes.

Everything about you was set in motion at that moment, by which sperm reached which egg.

</Step>

<Step title="The race to the egg" kind="interactive">

Hundreds of millions of sperm set out, but only **one** can fertilise the egg. Watch it happen.

<Fertilization />

When one sperm fuses with the egg, the egg instantly **seals its surface** so no other sperm can enter. The two nuclei join: **23 + 23 = 46** chromosomes — the full set for a new individual.

</Step>

<Step title="Why every child is unique" kind="explain">

Fertilisation is where variation is sealed in, through two layers of chance:

- **Which gametes:** thanks to meiosis (crossing over and independent assortment), every sperm and every egg is genetically different.
- **Which combination:** any one of millions of sperm could fertilise the egg.

Multiply those together and the number of genetically distinct children two parents *could* have is astronomical — which is why no two siblings (apart from identical twins) are the same.

<Callout type="tip" title="Identical vs non-identical twins">
**Identical** twins come from one zygote that splits in two — same genes. **Non-identical** twins come from two eggs fertilised by two sperm — no more alike than ordinary siblings.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Fertilisation** = the fusion of a **sperm** and an **egg** to form a single **zygote**.
- Only **one** sperm gets in; the egg then seals to block the rest, keeping the chromosome count at **46** (23 + 23).
- **Variation** comes from which gametes form (meiosis) and which combine — so every child is genetically unique.

</Step>
