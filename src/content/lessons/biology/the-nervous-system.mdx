<Step title="Staying in control" kind="explain">

To survive, an organism must sense its surroundings and respond — dodge a threat, find food, react to heat or light. Your body has two systems for this **coordination**: the fast, electrical **nervous system** (this world's main subject) and the slower, chemical **hormone** system.

The nervous system is your body's high-speed communication network, carrying signals as electrical impulses in a fraction of a second.

</Step>

<Step title="The response pathway" kind="interactive">

<NervousSystem />

A **stimulus** is detected by a **receptor**; a **sensory neuron** carries the message to the **central nervous system** (CNS), which coordinates a response; a **motor neuron** carries the instruction to an **effector**, which acts.

</Step>

<Step title="Central and peripheral" kind="explain">

The nervous system has two parts:

- The **central nervous system (CNS)** — the **brain** and **spinal cord** — the control centre that processes information and decides on responses.
- The **peripheral nervous system (PNS)** — all the **nerves** branching out to the rest of the body, carrying signals to and from the CNS.

Receptors and effectors are the system's interface with the world: **receptors** (in eyes, ears, skin) detect changes, and **effectors** (muscles and glands) carry out responses.

<Callout type="info" title="Two effectors">
There are only two kinds of effector: **muscles**, which respond by contracting (moving you), and **glands**, which respond by releasing a substance (like sweat or saliva).
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- The **nervous system** coordinates fast responses using electrical impulses.
- The pathway: **stimulus → receptor → (sensory neuron) → CNS → (motor neuron) → effector → response**.
- The **CNS** = brain + spinal cord; the **PNS** = the nerves to the body.
- **Effectors** are **muscles** (contract) or **glands** (secrete).

</Step>
