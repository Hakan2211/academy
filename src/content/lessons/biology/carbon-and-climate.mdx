<Lesson>

<Step title="One element, the whole story" kind="explain">

We finish where biology meets the fate of the planet. A single element — **carbon** — ties together photosynthesis, respiration, food webs, fossil fuels, and the climate of the entire Earth. Follow it, and you see how the small-scale biology of a leaf scales up to a global crisis.

This is the capstone: the carbon cycle, how humans have knocked it out of balance, and why that's warming the world.

</Step>

<Step title="The cycle in balance" kind="interactive">

For most of Earth's history, carbon moved in a balanced loop.

<CarbonCycle />

**Photosynthesis** pulls CO₂ out of the air; **respiration** and **decomposition** put it back. Over millions of years, some carbon was locked away underground as **fossil fuels** — dead organisms that never fully decomposed. For a very long time, what came out roughly matched what went in, and atmospheric CO₂ stayed steady.

</Step>

<Step title="The greenhouse effect" kind="explain">

Why does CO₂ in the air matter for temperature? Because of the **greenhouse effect** — and a little of it is essential for life.

- Sunlight reaches Earth and warms the surface.
- The warm surface radiates heat back out as infrared.
- **Greenhouse gases** (CO₂, methane, water vapour) absorb some of that heat and re-radiate it, keeping it in — like a blanket around the planet.

Without any greenhouse effect, Earth would be a frozen ball at about −18°C. The problem isn't the effect itself — it's making it **stronger** by adding more greenhouse gases.

</Step>

<Step title="Breaking the balance" kind="interactive">

Now switch on human emissions and watch the cycle tip.

<CarbonCycle />

By **burning fossil fuels**, we unlock carbon that took millions of years to bury and dump it into the air in a couple of centuries — far faster than photosynthesis can reabsorb it. **Deforestation** makes it worse, removing the very trees that draw CO₂ down. So CO₂ accumulates, thickening the greenhouse blanket.

</Step>

<Step title="The evidence" kind="interactive">

This isn't a prediction — it's a measurement. Look at what's happened since the industrial revolution.

<ClimateGraph />

Atmospheric CO₂ has risen from about **285 ppm** to over **415 ppm** — higher than at any time in millions of years — and global temperature has climbed right alongside it. The link between rising CO₂ and rising temperature is exactly what the greenhouse physics predicts.

</Step>

<Step title="Consequences and responses" kind="explain">

A warmer planet means more than warmer days:

- **Melting ice** and rising sea levels, threatening coastal cities.
- **More extreme weather** — fiercer storms, droughts, floods, and heatwaves.
- **Shifting habitats** — species must move, adapt, or die; ecosystems and food webs are disrupted, accelerating biodiversity loss.

But the same biology that diagnoses the problem points to the cure. The solutions all work *with* the carbon cycle:

- **Burn less fossil carbon** — switch to clean energy (solar, wind).
- **Protect and plant forests and oceans** — they pull CO₂ back down by photosynthesis.
- **Reduce waste and emissions** across farming and industry.

</Step>

<Step title="The thread through all of biology" kind="explain">

Step back and see how far one idea has carried you. The **photosynthesis** in a single chloroplast, scaled up across every plant on Earth, sets the planet's carbon balance. The **respiration** in your mitochondria, multiplied by billions of organisms, returns it. Disturb that balance — by digging up ancient carbon and burning it — and you change the climate of the whole world.

This is the deepest lesson of biology: everything is connected, across every scale, from a molecule to the biosphere. The cell, the organism, the ecosystem, and the planet are one continuous, living system — and, now more than ever, understanding it is in our hands.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Carbon** links photosynthesis, respiration, fossil fuels, and the planet's climate.
- The natural carbon cycle was **balanced**; the **greenhouse effect** (essential in moderation) keeps Earth warm.
- **Burning fossil fuels** and **deforestation** add CO₂ faster than it can be reabsorbed, strengthening the greenhouse effect.
- The **evidence**: CO₂ and global temperature have risen together since the industrial revolution.
- The **solutions** work with the cycle — clean energy and protecting carbon-absorbing ecosystems. Across every scale, life is one connected system.

</Step>
