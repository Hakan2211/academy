<Step title="Moving the food around" kind="explain">

Photosynthesis happens in the **leaves**, but the whole plant needs that sugar — the roots, the growing tips, the developing fruits and seeds. So the plant must transport sugar from where it's made to where it's needed.

This is the job of the second transport tissue, the **phloem**, and the process is called **translocation**.

</Step>

<Step title="From source to sink" kind="interactive">

Watch sugar travel down the phloem from the leaf to the roots.

<PhloemTransport />

Biologists describe it as flowing from a **source** (where sugar is made or stored, like a leaf) to a **sink** (where it's used or stored, like a root, fruit, or growing bud). Unlike the xylem, the phloem can carry sugar **either way** — up or down — depending on where the plant needs it.

</Step>

<Step title="Two pipes, two jobs" kind="explain">

Plants have two separate transport tissues running side by side, and it's worth keeping them straight:

| | Xylem | Phloem |
|---|---|---|
| Carries | water + minerals | dissolved sugar |
| Direction | up only (root → leaf) | both ways (source → sink) |
| Driven by | transpiration (passive) | active transport (energy) |

The xylem flow is one-way and powered by evaporation; translocation in the phloem uses **energy** to load sugar in, so it can be directed wherever the plant needs it most — to a ripening fruit in summer, or to bursting buds in spring.

<Callout type="info" title="Why two systems?">
Water and sugar move in different directions, at different times, for different reasons — so plants evolved two dedicated pipelines rather than trying to do both jobs with one.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Phloem** carries dissolved **sugar** from a **source** (e.g. a leaf) to a **sink** (root, fruit, bud) — this is **translocation**.
- It can flow **both ways**, directed by the plant's needs, and uses **energy** (active transport).
- Contrast with **xylem**: water + minerals, **upward only**, driven passively by transpiration.

</Step>
