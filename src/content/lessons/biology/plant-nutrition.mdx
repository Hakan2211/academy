<Step title="More than sunlight" kind="explain">

Photosynthesis gives a plant sugar — its source of energy and carbon. But you can't build a whole plant from sugar alone. To make **proteins, DNA, chlorophyll,** and more, a plant also needs **minerals**: elements it absorbs as dissolved ions from the soil through its roots.

A plant short of a key mineral shows it — often in the colour and growth of its leaves.

</Step>

<Step title="The key minerals" kind="interactive">

Pick a mineral to see its job and what deficiency looks like.

<PlantNutrition />

The big three are **nitrogen** (for proteins and growth), **phosphorus** (for DNA and roots), and **potassium** (for enzymes, flowers, and fruit) — the "NPK" you'll see on fertiliser bags. **Magnesium** is needed to make chlorophyll.

</Step>

<Step title="Reading a sick plant" kind="explain">

Deficiencies have tell-tale signs, because each mineral has a specific role:

- **No nitrogen** → stunted growth and yellow older leaves (can't make enough protein).
- **No magnesium** → yellowing between the veins (can't make chlorophyll).
- **No phosphorus** → poor roots and purple leaves.
- **No potassium** → yellow, scorched leaf edges and poor flowering.

This is why farmers and gardeners use **fertilisers** — to replace minerals the soil has lost, especially nitrogen, which crops use heavily. Minerals are absorbed by **active transport** into the roots (often against a concentration gradient), which is why root cells are rich in mitochondria.

<Callout type="tip" title="Diagnosing from leaves">
Because each deficiency looks different, you can often diagnose what a plant is missing just by looking at its leaves — yellowing between veins points to magnesium, overall yellow-and-stunted points to nitrogen.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- Beyond sugar, plants need **minerals** absorbed from the soil (by **active transport** into the roots).
- **Nitrogen** → proteins/growth; **phosphorus** → DNA/roots; **potassium** → enzymes/flowers ("NPK"); **magnesium** → chlorophyll.
- **Deficiencies** show as specific leaf symptoms (e.g. yellowing between veins = magnesium).
- **Fertilisers** replace the minerals crops remove from the soil.

</Step>
