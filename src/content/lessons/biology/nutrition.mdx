<Lesson>

<Step title="You are what you eat" kind="explain">

Your body is built and powered entirely by what you eat and drink. Food supplies the **energy** to keep you running and the **raw materials** to grow and repair. But no single food has everything — which is why a **balanced diet** means eating the right mix.

There are seven things your body needs from food.

</Step>

<Step title="The seven nutrient groups" kind="interactive">

Tap each group to see what it does and where it comes from.

<NutrientGroups />

Three are needed in large amounts — **carbohydrates** (energy), **proteins** (growth and repair), and **fats** (stored energy and membranes). The other four — **vitamins, minerals, fibre,** and **water** — are needed in smaller amounts but are just as essential.

</Step>

<Step title="Balance and deficiency" kind="explain">

"Balanced" means matching your intake to your needs. Too little of something — or too much — causes problems:

- Too little **iron** → anaemia (not enough red blood cells).
- Too little **vitamin C** → scurvy; too little **vitamin D** → weak bones.
- Too little **fibre** → constipation.
- Too much **energy** (food) compared to what you use → stored as fat → obesity.

Your exact needs depend on your age, size, and activity — an athlete needs far more energy than someone resting.

<Callout type="tip" title="Energy in vs energy out">
Body mass is a balance: eat more energy than you spend and you store the surplus as fat; spend more than you eat and you draw on your reserves. Diet and exercise both shift the balance.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- Food provides **energy** and **raw materials** for growth and repair.
- A **balanced diet** supplies all **seven** nutrient groups: carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, minerals, fibre, and water.
- Deficiencies cause specific problems (iron → anaemia, vitamin C → scurvy, fibre → constipation).
- Body mass depends on the balance of energy **in vs out**.

</Step>
