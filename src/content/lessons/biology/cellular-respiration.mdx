<Step title="Unlocking the energy in food" kind="explain">

Photosynthesis banks the Sun's energy in glucose. **Respiration** is how every living cell — plant *and* animal — spends it. It breaks glucose down to release energy and uses that energy to recharge **ATP**.

This isn't the same as breathing. **Breathing** moves air in and out of your lungs; **respiration** is the chemical reaction happening inside every one of your cells, every second of your life.

</Step>

<Step title="Aerobic respiration" kind="interactive">

With **oxygen**, respiration runs in the **mitochondria** and releases a large amount of energy.

<CellularRespiration />

The word equation is:

<Formula tex="\text{glucose} + \text{oxygen} \rightarrow \text{carbon dioxide} + \text{water}" block />

Notice it's almost exactly photosynthesis run backwards — and it yields about **38 ATP** per glucose.

</Step>

<Step title="Why it needs oxygen and mitochondria" kind="explain">

Oxygen is what lets glucose be broken down **completely**, squeezing out the maximum energy. That's why:

- The reactions happen in the **mitochondria**, whose folded inner membranes pack in the machinery.
- Cells that need lots of energy — muscle, liver, nerve — are **stuffed with mitochondria**.
- You breathe to supply oxygen and remove the carbon dioxide that respiration produces.

The carbon dioxide you breathe out is the waste product of this very reaction, made in your cells and carried by your blood to your lungs.

<Callout type="tip" title="Breathing vs respiration">
Don't mix them up. Breathing is the muscle action that ventilates your lungs. Respiration is the cell-level reaction that releases energy. Breathing simply *supplies* respiration with oxygen.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Respiration** releases energy from glucose to recharge **ATP** — in every cell, not just during exercise.
- **Aerobic** respiration uses **oxygen**, happens in the **mitochondria**, and yields a lot of energy (~38 ATP): glucose + oxygen → carbon dioxide + water.
- **Breathing** ≠ respiration — breathing supplies the oxygen respiration needs.

</Step>
