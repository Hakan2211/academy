<Lesson>

<Step title="One economy, powered by the Sun" kind="explain">

Step back and the whole of biological energy fits into a single, elegant loop. **Photosynthesis** captures sunlight and stores it in glucose. **Respiration** releases it again to make **ATP**, the currency every cell spends. And the waste of one is the raw material of the other.

This deep-dive zooms out to see the complete energy economy of life — and the beautiful symmetry at its heart.

</Step>

<Step title="The two halves fit together" kind="interactive">

First, the bank: photosynthesis stores the Sun's energy as glucose, releasing oxygen.

<Photosynthesis />

Now look at respiration — and notice it's almost the same equation **run backwards**.

<CellularRespiration />

Photosynthesis: CO₂ + water → glucose + oxygen. Respiration: glucose + oxygen → CO₂ + water. The outputs of one are the inputs of the other.

</Step>

<Step title="A loop that spans the planet" kind="explain">

Put them together and you have a planet-sized cycle:

- Plants **photosynthesise**, locking solar energy into glucose and releasing the **oxygen** that animals breathe.
- Animals (and plants) **respire**, releasing energy from that glucose and breathing out the **carbon dioxide** that plants need.

The same carbon and oxygen atoms cycle round and round; only the **energy** flows one way — in from the Sun, out eventually as heat. Life doesn't recycle energy; it recycles matter and rides a one-way river of solar energy.

</Step>

<Step title="Why glucose, then ATP?" kind="interactive">

You might ask: if respiration makes energy, why the two-step dance through glucose *and* ATP? Because they play different roles.

<ATPCycle />

- **Glucose** is the **store** — a lot of energy in a stable molecule, good for keeping.
- **ATP** is the **cash** — small, quick packets the cell can spend instantly on any job.

Respiration is the bank teller, steadily converting the big note (glucose) into usable change (ATP) as the cell needs it.

</Step>

<Step title="Following the energy down" kind="explain">

Inside respiration, energy isn't released in one blast — that would be wasteful and dangerous. Glucose is taken apart in **small steps**, each releasing a little energy, captured by recharging ATP.

The grand finale, in the mitochondria, passes electrons down a chain (the **electron transport chain**), using their energy to pump protons and, ultimately, to make most of the ATP. Oxygen sits at the very end, accepting the spent electrons — which is precisely **why we need to breathe it**. No oxygen to catch the electrons, and the whole chain backs up and stops.

</Step>

<Step title="The economy in balance" kind="explain">

So the cell's energy economy has a clear structure:

- **Income:** sunlight, captured by photosynthesis into glucose.
- **Savings:** glucose (and starch, glycogen, fat) — long-term stores.
- **Spending money:** ATP — made by respiration, spent on everything.
- **The catch:** every transfer loses some energy as **heat**, so the Sun must keep paying in. Energy flows *through* life; it is never created or recycled.

Master this loop and you understand the engine of the entire living world — from a blade of grass to your own beating heart.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Photosynthesis** stores solar energy in glucose; **respiration** releases it to make **ATP** — they are near-reverse reactions.
- Their inputs and outputs feed each other in a planet-wide loop: **matter cycles, energy flows one way** from the Sun (and is lost as heat).
- **Glucose** is the energy store; **ATP** is the spendable currency respiration makes from it.
- Respiration releases energy in **small steps**, ending with the **electron transport chain**, where **oxygen** accepts the spent electrons — the reason you breathe.

</Step>
