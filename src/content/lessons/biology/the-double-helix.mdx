<Step title="The molecule that holds the plans" kind="explain">

Every cell in your body carries a complete set of instructions for building and running you — written in **DNA**. The same molecule stores the blueprints for a bacterium, an oak tree, and a blue whale. Cracking its structure in 1953 was one of the great moments in science, because the structure instantly revealed how it works.

The shape is the famous **double helix**: two strands twisted around each other like a spiral ladder.

</Step>

<Step title="Two strands, paired bases" kind="interactive">

Watch it turn. The two outer **backbones** spiral around rungs made of paired **bases**.

<DNAHelix />

There are four bases — **A, T, C, G** — and here's the key: they only pair one way. **A always pairs with T**, and **C always pairs with G**. This is **complementary base pairing**, and it's the secret to everything DNA does.

</Step>

<Step title="Why the pairing rule matters" kind="explain">

Because the pairing is fixed, the two strands are **mirror images** of each other. Know one strand and you automatically know the other:

> If one strand reads **A-T-G-C**, the other must read **T-A-C-G**.

That has two huge consequences, which fill the rest of this world:

- **Copying:** split the strands and each is a perfect template to rebuild its partner — so DNA can be copied exactly (the next lesson).
- **Coding:** the *order* of the bases along a strand is a message — the instructions for building proteins.

<Callout type="info" title="A four-letter language">
DNA stores information the way this sentence does — as a sequence of letters. With just four letters (A, T, C, G) in the right order, it spells out every protein your body can make.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **DNA** stores the instructions for life as a sequence of four bases: **A, T, C, G**.
- Its shape is the **double helix** — two backbones around paired bases.
- Bases pair by a fixed rule: **A–T** and **C–G** (complementary base pairing).
- This makes the strands mirror images — enabling DNA to be **copied** and to **code** for proteins.

</Step>
