<Step title="The body's slower messengers" kind="explain">

Nerves are fast but fleeting. For control that's slower but **longer-lasting and far-reaching**, the body uses **hormones** — chemical messengers carried in the **blood**.

Hormones are made by **glands** (together called the **endocrine system**), released into the bloodstream, and travel everywhere — but only affect the specific **target organs** built to respond to them.

</Step>

The **pituitary** is the "master gland" controlling the others. The **pancreas** controls blood sugar, the **thyroid** sets metabolism, the **adrenals** trigger fight-or-flight, and the **ovaries/testes** drive reproduction.

</Step>

<Step title="Nerves vs hormones" kind="explain">

The two control systems are a study in contrasts — and the body uses each where it fits best:

| | Nervous system | Hormones |
|---|---|---|
| Signal | electrical impulse | chemical in blood |
| Speed | very fast | slower |
| Duration | brief | longer-lasting |
| Spread | precise target | whole body |

Use **nerves** for split-second reactions (catching a ball); use **hormones** for sustained, body-wide changes (growth, puberty, the stress response, controlling blood sugar over hours).

<Callout type="tip" title="Adrenaline — fast for a hormone">
Adrenaline is a hormone, yet it acts quickly — flooding the blood when you're frightened to raise heart rate and breathing. It's the body's way of getting a fast, whole-body response from the chemical system.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Hormones** are chemical messengers made by **glands** (the **endocrine system**) and carried in the **blood** to target organs.
- Key glands: **pituitary** (master), **pancreas** (blood sugar), **thyroid** (metabolism), **adrenals** (adrenaline), **ovaries/testes** (reproduction).
- Compared with nerves, hormones are **slower, longer-lasting,** and **body-wide**.

</Step>
