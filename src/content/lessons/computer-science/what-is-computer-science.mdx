<Lesson>

<Step title="Not just coding" kind="explain">

Ask most people what computer science is and they'll say "programming." That's like saying astronomy is "looking through telescopes." Coding is a *tool* — computer science is the bigger idea behind it.

**Computer science is the study of problem-solving with computation:** how to represent information, how to process it step by step, and what can (and cannot) be done by following a precise set of instructions.

The surprising part: the core ideas existed *before* electronic computers. They're really about **information and procedure** — and a computer is just a machine fast and reliable enough to carry them out.

</Step>

<Step title="What every computer does" kind="interactive">

Underneath every app, game and website is the same simple loop: take some **input**, **process** it, produce **output** — and optionally **store** the result. Pick a task and watch the four parts do the work.

<ComputerModel />

Whether it's adding numbers or editing a photo, the parts never change — only what flows through them does. That's the universal model of a computer.

</Step>

<Step title="The two questions of CS" kind="explain">

Computer scientists keep returning to two questions:

- **How do we solve this with a clear procedure?** Designing step-by-step methods — *algorithms* — is the heart of the field.
- **How do we represent the information?** Numbers, text, images, sound, relationships — everything must become patterns a machine can store and manipulate.

<Callout type="tip" title="The big skill">
The real superpower of CS is **abstraction**: hiding detail so you can think at the right level. You can write an email without knowing how the screen lights up — because each layer hides the one below it.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Why it's everywhere" kind="explain">

Computation now runs medicine, money, music, maps, science and conversation. The same foundations power a calculator and a model that writes poetry. Learning computer science means learning the rules that this whole digital world plays by — and how to bend them to solve new problems.

In this island you'll build that understanding from the ground up: from a single bit, to logic gates, to algorithms, to the internet, to artificial intelligence, all the way to the deep question of what computers *cannot* do.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Computer science** is the study of solving problems with computation — not just coding.
- Every computer follows one loop: **input → process → output**, with optional **storage**.
- The two recurring questions are how to **represent information** and how to design **algorithms**.
- **Abstraction** — hiding detail to think at the right level — is the central skill.

</Step>
