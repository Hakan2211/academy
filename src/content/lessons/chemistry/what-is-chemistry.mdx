<Step title="The science of stuff" kind="explain">

Look around you. The screen you're reading, the air you're breathing, the water you drink, the cells in your body — all of it is **matter**: anything that has mass and takes up space.

**Chemistry** is the science of matter — what it's made of, how it behaves, and how one substance turns into another. If physics asks how things *move*, chemistry asks what things *are* and how they *change*.

Every material in the universe, from a diamond to a thundercloud, is built from about a hundred basic ingredients combined in different ways. Chemistry is the story of those ingredients.

</Step>

Don't worry about the labels yet — we'll unpack *element*, *compound*, and *mixture* across this world. For now, notice the key idea: **what something is depends on its tiny particles and how they're arranged.**

</Step>

<Step title="The three big questions" kind="explain">

Chemists keep coming back to three questions:

- **What is it made of?** Break any material down far enough and you reach atoms — the subject of the next world.
- **Why does it behave that way?** Why does salt dissolve, metal conduct, or wood burn? The answer is always in the particles.
- **How can we change it?** Reactions rearrange atoms into new substances — turning ore into metal, oil into plastic, food into energy.

<Callout type="tip" title="The chemist's superpower">
Chemistry connects two worlds: the **macroscopic** stuff you can see and hold, and the **microscopic** particles you can't. Learning to flip between these two views is the single most important skill in the whole subject.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Why it matters" kind="explain">

Chemistry isn't locked in a lab. It's the medicine that cures you, the fertiliser that feeds billions, the battery in your phone, the materials in every building, and the reactions that power your own body second by second.

Understanding chemistry means understanding the rules that everything physical plays by.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Matter** is anything with mass that takes up space.
- **Chemistry** is the science of matter — its composition, behaviour, and transformations.
- Everything is built from about a hundred basic ingredients (the elements).
- The core skill is connecting the **macroscopic** world you see with the **microscopic** particles that explain it.
