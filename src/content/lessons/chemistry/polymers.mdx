<Step title="Giant molecules from small repeats" kind="explain">

A **polymer** is a giant molecule built by linking thousands of small repeating units called **monomers** — like a chain made of identical links. The word means "many parts."

Polymers are everywhere: plastics, rubber, nylon, and the natural polymers of life — starch, cellulose, proteins, and DNA. Carbon's ability to chain makes them all possible.

</Step>

<Step title="Two ways to polymerise" kind="explain">

There are two main routes:

- **Addition polymerisation** — unsaturated monomers (with C=C double bonds) open up and join with **nothing lost**. Many ethene molecules → poly(ethene). Most common plastics form this way.
- **Condensation polymerisation** — monomers join by reacting functional groups, **expelling a small molecule** (usually water) each time. This builds nylon, polyester, and proteins.

In both, small repeating units chain into a single enormous molecule.

</Step>

<Step title="Why polymers matter — and their problem" kind="explain">

Polymers transformed the modern world: cheap, light, strong, mouldable materials for packaging, clothing, construction, and medicine.

But the same stability that makes plastics useful makes many of them **non-biodegradable** — they persist for centuries, polluting land and oceans. Chemists now work on **biodegradable** and **recyclable** polymers to address it.

<Callout type="info" title="Life is built from polymers too">
Your body is a polymer factory. Proteins are polymers of amino acids; DNA is a polymer of nucleotides; starch and cellulose are polymers of glucose. The next world — biochemistry — is the chemistry of these natural polymers of life.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- A **polymer** is a giant molecule of many repeating **monomer** units.
- **Addition** polymerisation joins unsaturated monomers (C=C opens) with nothing lost; **condensation** joins them by expelling a small molecule.
- Polymers gave us plastics and synthetics — but many are **non-biodegradable**, a real pollution problem.
- **Life's molecules** (proteins, DNA, starch) are natural polymers — the bridge to biochemistry.

</Step>
