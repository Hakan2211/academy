<Lesson>

<Step title="There's a limit" kind="explain">

You can't dissolve sugar forever — eventually it just sits at the bottom. **Solubility** is the maximum amount of a solute that will dissolve in a given amount of solvent at a given temperature.

- **Unsaturated** — below the limit; more can still dissolve.
- **Saturated** — exactly at the limit; no more will dissolve.
- **Supersaturated** — temporarily holding *more* than the limit (unstable); the excess crystallises at the slightest disturbance.

</Step>

<Step title="The solubility curve" kind="interactive">

Solubility usually changes with temperature. The curve shows the saturation point at each temperature — on the line is saturated, below is unsaturated.

<Step title="Temperature: solids vs gases" kind="explain">

Here's a striking split:

- For most **solids**, solubility **rises** with temperature — hot water dissolves far more sugar or potassium nitrate.
- For **gases**, solubility **falls** with temperature — warm soda goes flat because it holds less CO₂, and warm rivers hold less dissolved oxygen for fish.

So heating helps dissolve a solid but drives a gas *out* of solution.

</Step>

<Step title="Pressure and gases" kind="explain">

Pressure barely affects solids and liquids, but it strongly affects **gases**: higher pressure forces more gas to dissolve.

A fizzy drink is bottled under high CO₂ pressure. Open it and the pressure drops, so the gas can no longer stay dissolved — it escapes as bubbles. That's the *fizz*.

<Callout type="tip" title="Growing crystals">
Make a hot saturated solution, then cool it slowly. As it cools, solubility drops below the dissolved amount, and the excess solute crystallises out — exactly how rock candy and many lab crystals are grown.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Solubility** is the maximum solute that dissolves at a given temperature: **unsaturated / saturated / supersaturated**.
- The **solubility curve** maps saturation against temperature.
- Most **solids** dissolve more when hot; **gases** dissolve *less* when hot.
- **Pressure** strongly increases **gas** solubility (the fizz in a sealed drink).

</Step>
