<Lesson>

<Step title="Atoms are never created or destroyed" kind="explain">

In any reaction, atoms are only **rearranged** — never made or lost. So every atom that goes *in* (reactants) must come *out* (products). This is the **law of conservation of mass**, and a **balanced equation** is how we honour it.

A balanced equation has the **same number of each kind of atom on both sides** of the arrow.

</Step>

<Step title="Balance it yourself" kind="interactive">

You can only change the **coefficients** — the big numbers in front — never the subscripts inside a formula. Adjust the coefficients until every element tallies on both sides.

<EquationBalancer />

</Step>

<Step title="A reliable method" kind="explain">

When balancing by hand:

1. Write the correct formulas — and **don't touch the subscripts**.
2. Balance one element at a time, saving **oxygen and hydrogen for last**.
3. Use coefficients to even up each element.
4. If you get a fraction, multiply everything to clear it.
5. Check that every atom balances at the end.

</Step>

<Step title="Why subscripts are off-limits" kind="explain">

Changing a subscript would change the *substance*. If H₂O won't balance, you can't "fix" it to H₂O₂ — that's hydrogen peroxide, a different chemical. The formulas are facts about what the molecules *are*; only the coefficients (how many) are yours to adjust.

<Callout type="tip" title="Balanced ≠ finished reaction">
A balanced equation describes the *ratio* in which substances react, not whether the reaction goes to completion or how fast. Those come later (kinetics and equilibrium). Balancing is purely the bookkeeping of atoms.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- A reaction only **rearranges** atoms (**conservation of mass**), so equations must be **balanced**.
- Balance by changing **coefficients** only — never subscripts.
- Method: correct formulas → balance element by element (H and O last) → clear fractions → check.
- A balanced equation gives the **reacting ratio**, not the speed or completeness.

</Step>
