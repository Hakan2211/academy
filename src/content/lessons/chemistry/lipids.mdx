<Step title="Fats, oils, and membranes" kind="explain">

**Lipids** are the fats and oils — and the membranes of every cell. What unites them is being **non-polar** (hydrophobic): they don't dissolve in water. That single property defines what they do.

They're built from **fatty acids** — long hydrocarbon tails with a –COOH acid head, straight from the organic world.

</Step>

</Step>

<Step title="Triglycerides: the energy store" kind="explain">

A **triglyceride** — the fat in food and body — is **glycerol** joined to **three fatty-acid tails** (by condensation, releasing water). Those long hydrocarbon tails are packed with C–H bonds, making fats the body's most **energy-dense** store, more than twice as much per gram as carbohydrate.

**Saturated** fats (no C=C, straight tails) pack tightly and are solid (butter, lard). **Unsaturated** fats (with C=C, kinked tails) can't pack as well and are liquid oils (olive, sunflower) — the alkene chemistry from before, on your plate.

</Step>

<Step title="Phospholipids: the membrane" kind="explain">

Swap one fatty-acid tail for a **phosphate group** and you get a **phospholipid** — a molecule with a water-*loving* head and water-*hating* tails. In water, these self-assemble tails-inward into a **bilayer**: the membrane around every cell.

<Callout type="info" title="Like dissolves like, building life">
The phospholipid bilayer is "like dissolves like" turned into structure. The non-polar tails huddle away from water while the polar heads face it — a self-forming barrier that's the foundation of every cell. Polarity, from the bonding world, literally builds the boundary of life.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Lipids** are non-polar fats, oils, and membranes — defined by not dissolving in water.
- **Triglycerides** (glycerol + 3 fatty acids) are the energy-dense store; **saturated** = solid, **unsaturated** = liquid oils.
- **Phospholipids** (a phosphate head + tails) self-assemble into the membrane **bilayer**.
- Polarity from the bonding world literally builds the boundary of the cell.

</Step>
