<Step title="The information molecules" kind="explain">

The fourth family stores and carries life's **information**: the **nucleic acids**, DNA and RNA. They're polymers of **nucleotides**, and their job is to hold the instructions for building every protein.

Each **nucleotide** has three parts: a **sugar**, a **phosphate** group, and a nitrogen-containing **base**. The bases come in four kinds — and their *sequence* is the genetic code.

</Step>

<Step title="The double helix" kind="interactive">

DNA's nucleotides chain into two strands twisted into the famous **double helix**.

<DNAHelix />

</Step>

<Step title="Base pairing holds it together" kind="explain">

The two strands are held together by **hydrogen bonds** between paired bases — and the pairing is exact: **A with T**, **C with G**. (In RNA, uracil U replaces T.)

This complementary pairing is the chemical genius of DNA: each strand is a template for the other, so the molecule can be **copied** precisely every time a cell divides. Hydrogen bonding — from the bonding world — is what makes heredity work.

</Step>

<Step title="From sequence to protein" kind="explain">

The order of bases along DNA is a **code**. Read in groups of three, it spells out the order of amino acids in a protein. So the information flows:

**DNA → RNA → protein.**

The chemistry of four simple bases, in sequence, ultimately specifies every protein — and therefore every trait — of a living thing.

<Callout type="info" title="Chemistry becomes biology">
Here chemistry hands off to biology. The phosphate–sugar backbone, the hydrogen-bonded base pairs, the condensation polymerisation — all pure chemistry — add up to the molecule of heredity. The molecules of life are where this island meets the Biology island.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Nucleic acids** (DNA, RNA) store information; they're polymers of **nucleotides** (sugar + phosphate + base).
- DNA is a **double helix**; strands are held by **hydrogen bonds** with exact base pairing **A–T, C–G**.
- Complementary pairing lets DNA be **copied** precisely — the chemistry of heredity.
- The base **sequence** is a code: **DNA → RNA → protein** — where chemistry becomes biology.

</Step>
