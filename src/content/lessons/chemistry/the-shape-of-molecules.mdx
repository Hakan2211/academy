<Lesson>

<Step title="From bond to behaviour" kind="explain">

You now have the full chain of reasoning that turns *atoms* into *materials with properties*. Let's connect it end to end:

**electrons → bond type → Lewis structure → shape → polarity → forces between molecules → physical properties.**

Every link follows from the one before. Master this chain and you can reason from an element's place on the periodic table all the way to whether a substance is a gas, a liquid, or a rock.

</Step>

<Step title="Shape sets up polarity" kind="interactive">

Start with shape. The geometry decides whether a molecule's bond polarities cancel or combine.

<VSEPRViewer />

A symmetric shape (linear CO₂, tetrahedral CH₄) cancels its polarities → non-polar. An asymmetric shape (bent H₂O, pyramidal NH₃) leaves a net dipole → polar.

</Step>

<Step title="Polarity sets up the forces" kind="interactive">

Polarity then decides how strongly molecules cling to *each other*.

<Polarity />

Non-polar molecules only have weak dispersion forces; polar molecules add dipole attractions; and when H meets N, O, or F, hydrogen bonding makes the strongest pull of all.

</Step>

<Step title="The payoff: predicting properties" kind="explain">

Now read off the consequences:

- **Stronger intermolecular forces → higher melting and boiling points.** Water (hydrogen-bonded) boils at 100 °C; methane (dispersion only) boils at −161 °C — despite similar size.
- **"Like dissolves like."** Polar solvents (water) dissolve polar and ionic substances; non-polar solvents (oil) dissolve non-polar substances. This is why oil and water refuse to mix.
- **Shape enables recognition.** Enzymes, receptors, and drugs work because one specific shape fits another like a key in a lock.

</Step>

<Step title="The whole chain at once" kind="explain">

Take water as the worked example:

1. O and H are non-metals → **covalent** bonds.
2. Lewis structure: 2 bonds + 2 lone pairs on oxygen.
3. Four electron regions + 2 lone pairs → **bent** shape (~104.5°).
4. Bent + electronegative O → a **polar** molecule.
5. H bonded to O → **hydrogen bonding** between molecules.
6. Result: high boiling point, powerful solvent, ice floats, life is possible.

<Callout type="info" title="One idea, all of chemistry">
This is the deep reward of the bonding world: the macroscopic properties you can see and measure are *downstream* of invisible electrons and shapes. Change the bonding and you change the material. Next, we start counting these particles to predict exactly how much reacts with how much.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- The reasoning chain: **electrons → bond type → Lewis structure → shape → polarity → intermolecular forces → properties.**
- **Shape** (VSEPR) decides whether bond polarities cancel; **polarity** decides the strength of forces between molecules.
- Stronger forces → higher melting/boiling points; **"like dissolves like"** governs solubility; shape enables biological recognition.
- A substance's visible properties are **downstream** of invisible electrons and geometry.

</Step>
