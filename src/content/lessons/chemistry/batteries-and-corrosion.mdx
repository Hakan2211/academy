<Lesson>

<Step title="Electrochemistry you can hold" kind="explain">

Two of electrochemistry's biggest real-world stories are opposites: **batteries** (redox we *want*, harnessed for power) and **corrosion** (redox we *don't* want, destroying metal). Both are galvanic cells in action.

</Step>

<Step title="A battery is a packaged cell" kind="interactive">

Every battery is a galvanic cell (or several in series): a spontaneous redox reaction whose electrons we route through a device.

<GalvanicCell />

- A **single-use** cell runs until its reactants are spent.
- A **rechargeable** battery reverses the reaction by electrolysis when you charge it — running the redox backwards to restock the reactants.

</Step>

<Step title="Corrosion: unwanted redox" kind="explain">

**Corrosion** is metal being oxidised by its environment. The most familiar is **rusting**: iron reacts with oxygen and water to form hydrated iron oxide (rust), flaking away and weakening the metal.

It's the same electron-transfer chemistry as a battery — but here it's slowly eating bridges, cars, and pipes, costing economies billions every year.

</Step>

<Step title="Fighting corrosion" kind="explain">

Knowing the chemistry suggests the defences:

- **Barriers** — paint, oil, or plating block oxygen and water from reaching the metal.
- **Galvanising** — coating iron with more-reactive zinc, which oxidises *instead* of the iron.
- **Sacrificial protection** — bolting a block of a more reactive metal (zinc or magnesium) to a ship's hull or pipeline; it corrodes preferentially, sparing the steel.

<Callout type="tip" title="Sacrifice by reactivity">
Sacrificial protection works straight from the activity series: a more reactive metal gives up its electrons more readily, so it's oxidised first and protects the metal you care about. The reactivity ranking you met earlier pays off here.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Batteries** are galvanic cells delivering power; **rechargeable** ones reverse the reaction by electrolysis to recharge.
- **Corrosion** (e.g. rusting) is unwanted oxidation of metal by oxygen and water.
- Defences: **barriers**, **galvanising**, and **sacrificial protection**.
- Sacrificial protection uses a **more reactive metal** (activity series) to corrode in place of the protected one.

</Step>
