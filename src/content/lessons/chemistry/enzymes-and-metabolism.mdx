<Step title="Life's catalysts" kind="explain">

A cell runs thousands of reactions, and almost none would happen fast enough at body temperature on their own. The answer is **enzymes** — biological **catalysts**, almost all of them proteins.

Like any catalyst, an enzyme works by **lowering the activation energy** of a reaction, so it speeds the reaction up enormously without being used up. Some enzymes accelerate reactions by *millions* of times.

</Step>

<Step title="Lock and key" kind="interactive">

Each enzyme has a specially shaped pocket — the **active site** — that fits one particular substrate, like a lock and key.

<EnzymeLab />

</Step>

<Step title="Why specificity and conditions matter" kind="explain">

Because the active site has a precise **shape**, each enzyme works on just one reaction — exactly the structure-determines-function theme from the protein lesson.

And because that shape is delicate, enzymes are sensitive to conditions:

- **Temperature** — a sweet spot (around body temperature); too hot and the enzyme **denatures**.
- **pH** — each enzyme has an optimum; this is why your body buffers pH so carefully.

The kinetics, catalysis, and acid–base ideas from earlier worlds all converge here.

</Step>

<Step title="Metabolism: chemistry organised" kind="explain">

**Metabolism** is the entire network of an organism's reactions, each step run by its own enzyme:

- **Catabolism** breaks molecules down to release energy (respiration breaking down glucose).
- **Anabolism** builds molecules up, using energy (making proteins from amino acids).

Energy is shuttled between them by **ATP**, the cell's energy currency — the thermochemistry of this island, running inside you.

<Callout type="info" title="The whole island, alive">
Look back: bonding builds the molecules, energetics powers them, kinetics and catalysis set the pace, acid–base and redox do the work, and organic chemistry supplies the structures. Metabolism is *all of chemistry* organised into the process we call life.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Enzymes** are biological **catalysts** (mostly proteins) that lower **activation energy** without being consumed.
- A precisely shaped **active site** makes each enzyme specific (lock and key) — and sensitive to **temperature and pH** (denaturation).
- **Metabolism** = the whole reaction network: **catabolism** (break down, release energy) + **anabolism** (build up, use energy), linked by **ATP**.
- Metabolism is all of chemistry organised into life.

</Step>
