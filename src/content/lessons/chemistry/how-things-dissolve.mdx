<Step title="Like dissolves like" kind="explain">

Why does salt dissolve in water but not in oil? The golden rule is **like dissolves like**:

- **Polar** solvents (like water) dissolve **polar** and **ionic** substances.
- **Non-polar** solvents (like oil) dissolve **non-polar** substances.

A solvent dissolves a solute when the attractions *between* them are as strong as the attractions holding each one together. Mismatch the polarity and there's no pull to pry the solute apart.

</Step>

<Step title="Polarity is the key" kind="interactive">

Recall what makes a molecule polar — it's the same property that decides what dissolves what.

<Polarity />

Water's strong polarity is exactly why it's such a powerful solvent for ionic and polar substances.

</Step>

<Step title="How water dissolves salt" kind="interactive">

Water molecules are little dipoles. Their negative (oxygen) ends cluster around positive ions, and their positive (hydrogen) ends around negative ions, prying each ion off the crystal and surrounding it — a process called **hydration**.

<DissolveLab />

</Step>

<Step title="Why oil and water won't mix" kind="explain">

Oil molecules are non-polar — water has no charged end to grab onto them, and the oil offers nothing for water's dipoles to attach to. Water molecules would rather hydrogen-bond to *each other* than make room for oil, so the two separate into layers. They're **immiscible**.

<Callout type="info" title="Soap bridges the gap">
Soap molecules have a polar (water-loving) head and a non-polar (oil-loving) tail. One end grabs grease, the other grabs water — letting the two finally mix and wash away. It's "like dissolves like" engineered to defeat itself.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Like dissolves like**: polar/ionic solutes dissolve in polar solvents; non-polar in non-polar.
- Dissolving happens when **solute–solvent attractions** can overcome the attractions within each.
- Water dissolves ionic solids by **hydration** — dipoles surrounding each ion.
- Oil and water are **immiscible**; soap bridges polar and non-polar to mix them.

</Step>
