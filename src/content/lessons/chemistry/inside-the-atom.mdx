<Step title="Three particles" kind="explain">

The atom turned out to be divisible after all — it's built from three **subatomic particles**:

- **Protons** — positive charge (+1), found in the nucleus. Heavy.
- **Neutrons** — no charge, also in the nucleus. Heavy.
- **Electrons** — negative charge (−1), whizzing around the nucleus. Almost massless (about 1/1800 of a proton).

The protons and neutrons huddle in the dense central **nucleus**; the electrons occupy the vast space around it.

</Step>

<Step title="Build an atom" kind="interactive">

Add protons, neutrons, and electrons and watch the atom take shape. Notice how the number of **protons** decides *which element* you've built, while electrons and neutrons change its charge and mass.

<AtomBuilder />

</Step>

<Step title="Mostly empty space" kind="explain">

If the nucleus were the size of a marble, the nearest electron would be about a kilometre away. An atom is overwhelmingly **empty space** — the solidity of everything you touch is really electrons repelling other electrons.

Almost all the **mass** sits in the nucleus (protons + neutrons), while almost all the **volume** is the electron cloud.

</Step>

<Step title="Charge and neutrality" kind="explain">

In a neutral atom, the number of protons equals the number of electrons, so the +1s and −1s cancel:

- Same number of protons and electrons → **neutral atom**.
- More electrons than protons → negative **ion**.
- Fewer electrons than protons → positive **ion**.

<Callout type="tip" title="Protons define identity">
You can add or remove electrons (making ions) or neutrons (making isotopes) and it's *still the same element*. Change the **proton** count and you've changed the element itself. Protons are the atom's identity badge.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- Atoms are built from **protons** (+), **neutrons** (0), and **electrons** (−).
- Protons and neutrons sit in the dense **nucleus**; electrons occupy the surrounding space.
- Almost all the **mass** is in the nucleus; almost all the **volume** is the electron cloud — the atom is mostly empty.
- The **proton count** defines the element; electrons (ions) and neutrons (isotopes) can vary.

</Step>
