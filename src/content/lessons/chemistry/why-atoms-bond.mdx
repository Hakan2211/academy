<Step title="The drive for a full shell" kind="explain">

Almost nothing in nature exists as lone atoms — they clump into molecules and crystals. Why? Because atoms are more **stable** with a full outer electron shell, and bonding is how they get one.

The noble gases already have full shells, which is exactly why they *don't* bond. Every other atom chases that same stable arrangement — usually eight outer electrons, the **octet rule** — by sharing, giving, or taking electrons.

</Step>

<Step title="Reaching stability" kind="interactive">

Watch how an atom completes its outer shell by losing or gaining electrons. A bond is just two atoms solving this problem *together*.

<IonFormation />

</Step>

<Step title="Three ways to bond" kind="explain">

There are three strategies, and which one happens depends on the atoms involved:

- **Ionic** — a metal *gives* electrons to a non-metal. The resulting opposite ions attract. (metal + non-metal)
- **Covalent** — two non-metals *share* electrons so both count a full shell. (non-metal + non-metal)
- **Metallic** — many metal atoms *pool* their outer electrons into a shared sea. (metal + metal)

In every case the goal is identical: full outer shells, lower energy, greater stability.

</Step>

<Step title="Bonding releases energy" kind="explain">

A bonded arrangement sits at **lower energy** than the separate atoms — that's what "more stable" means. Forming a bond *releases* energy; breaking one *costs* energy. This single fact drives all of chemistry's energy changes, which we'll explore in a later world.

<Callout type="tip" title="Predict the bond from the table">
The periodic table tells you which bond to expect. Two non-metals (top right)? Covalent. A metal (left) meeting a non-metal (right)? Ionic. All metals? Metallic. The next lessons take each in turn.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- Atoms bond to reach a stable **full outer shell** (the octet rule) — the arrangement noble gases already have.
- Three strategies: **ionic** (give/take), **covalent** (share), **metallic** (pool).
- Which one occurs depends on whether the atoms are metals or non-metals.
- Forming bonds **releases** energy (more stable); breaking them **costs** energy.

</Step>
