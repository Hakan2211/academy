<Step title="The great divide" kind="explain">

The most basic split in the table runs as a staircase line on the right side. It separates the two great families of elements:

- **Metals** (left and centre, the large majority) — shiny, conduct heat and electricity, bendable (malleable), and tend to **lose** electrons.
- **Non-metals** (top right) — dull, brittle, poor conductors, and tend to **gain** electrons.
- **Metalloids** (along the staircase) — in-between elements with a mix of both behaviours.

</Step>

<Step title="Why the behaviours differ" kind="explain">

It all comes back to valence electrons:

- Metals have **few** valence electrons that are loosely held — easy to give away, which is why they conduct (the electrons roam freely) and form positive ions.
- Non-metals have **nearly full** outer shells — they hold their electrons tightly and grab more, forming negative ions.

The staircase is really the boundary where "easily loses electrons" tips over into "easily gains electrons."

</Step>

<Step title="The metalloids in between" kind="explain">

Elements right on the staircase — boron, silicon, germanium, arsenic, antimony, tellurium — are **metalloids** (semi-metals). They're neither clearly metal nor non-metal.

This in-between nature is wildly useful. **Silicon** is a *semiconductor*: it conducts electricity only sometimes, which is exactly the controllable switch that every computer chip is built from.

<Callout type="info" title="A trend within the divide">
Metallic character increases as you go **down and to the left** (toward caesium, the most metallic) and decreases **up and to the right** (toward fluorine, the most non-metallic). The staircase is the crossover.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- A **staircase line** splits **metals** (most of the table) from **non-metals** (top right).
- Metals **lose** electrons (shiny, conductive, malleable); non-metals **gain** electrons (dull, brittle, insulating).
- **Metalloids** straddle the line with mixed properties — silicon's semiconductor behaviour powers all electronics.
- Metallic character rises **down and left**, falls **up and right**.

</Step>
