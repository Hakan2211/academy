<Step title="Turning a reaction into electricity" kind="explain">

If electrons flow during a redox reaction, why not route them through a wire and do useful work? That's exactly what a **galvanic cell** (or voltaic cell) does — it harnesses a **spontaneous** redox reaction to generate **electricity**. Every battery is a galvanic cell.

The trick is to *separate* the two half-reactions so the electrons are forced to travel through an external wire to get from one to the other.

</Step>

<Step title="Anode, cathode, and the salt bridge" kind="explain">

Three parts do the work:

- The **anode** is where **oxidation** happens — it releases electrons (the negative terminal in a galvanic cell).
- The **cathode** is where **reduction** happens — it accepts electrons (the positive terminal).
- The **salt bridge** lets ions flow between the half-cells to keep each side electrically neutral, so the reaction can keep going.

Electrons travel **anode → cathode** through the wire; that current is the electricity you use.

<Callout type="tip" title="A mnemonic">
**An Ox** and a **Red Cat**: **An**ode = **Ox**idation; **Red**uction = **Cat**hode. It holds for every electrochemical cell.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Cell voltage" kind="explain">

The cell produces a **voltage** — the "push" on the electrons — set by the difference in how strongly each half-reaction pulls electrons. A bigger difference in reactivity between the two metals means a higher voltage. The zinc–copper cell delivers about **1.10 V**.

Stack cells in series and the voltages add — which is how a 1.5 V cell becomes a 9 V battery.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- A **galvanic (voltaic) cell** harnesses a **spontaneous** redox reaction to make electricity — every battery is one.
- **Anode** = oxidation (− terminal); **cathode** = reduction (+ terminal): *An Ox, Red Cat*.
- The **salt bridge** keeps the half-cells neutral so current keeps flowing.
- The **cell voltage** depends on the reactivity difference; cells in series add their voltages.

</Step>
