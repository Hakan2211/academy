<Lesson>

<Step title="Strength is about ionising" kind="explain">

"Strong" and "weak" describe **how completely** an acid or base splits into ions in water — *not* how concentrated it is. This is one of chemistry's most common confusions, so pin it down:

- A **strong acid** ionises almost **completely** — nearly every molecule donates its H⁺ (HCl, H₂SO₄, HNO₃).
- A **weak acid** ionises only **partially** — most molecules stay intact, releasing few H⁺ (acetic acid in vinegar, citric acid).

</Step>

<Step title="Watch them ionise" kind="interactive">

Compare a strong and a weak acid at the same concentration. The strong one fills the solution with ions; the weak one mostly stays as whole molecules.

<Dissociation />

</Step>

<Step title="Strength vs concentration" kind="explain">

These are two completely separate ideas:

- **Strength** = the *fraction* that ionises (a property of the substance).
- **Concentration** = how *much* acid is dissolved (moles per litre).

You can have **dilute strong acid** (little HCl, but it all ionises) or **concentrated weak acid** (lots of vinegar, but little of it ionises). A dilute strong acid can even have a *lower* pH than a concentrated weak one.

</Step>

<Step title="Why it matters" kind="explain">

Strength explains everyday safety and behaviour. Vinegar (weak) is safe on chips; hydrochloric acid (strong) at the same concentration would burn — because it floods the solution with H⁺. Weak acids also resist pH change (the basis of buffers, coming in the capstone).

<Callout type="tip" title="Same idea for bases">
Strong bases (NaOH, KOH) fully release OH⁻; weak bases (ammonia) only partially. Strength is about the *degree of ionisation* for both acids and bases.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Strength** = how completely an acid/base **ionises** (strong = nearly fully; weak = partly).
- This is separate from **concentration** (how much is dissolved).
- You can have dilute strong acids and concentrated weak acids — strength ≠ concentration.
- Strong acids/bases flood solution with H⁺/OH⁻; weak ones don't (and underlie buffers).

</Step>
