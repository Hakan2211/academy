<Lesson>

<Step title="Burning is a reaction with oxygen" kind="explain">

**Combustion** is a rapid reaction between a fuel and **oxygen** that releases lots of energy as **heat and light** — a fire. It powers engines, stoves, power stations, and (more gently) your own metabolism.

Most fuels are **hydrocarbons** — compounds of just hydrogen and carbon, like methane (CH₄), propane, petrol, and wax.

</Step>

<Step title="The combustion pattern" kind="interactive">

When a hydrocarbon burns completely, the carbon ends up as carbon dioxide and the hydrogen as water:

<ReactionTypes />

The general recipe: **hydrocarbon + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O + energy.**

</Step>

<Step title="Complete vs incomplete" kind="explain">

How a fuel burns depends on how much oxygen is available:

- **Complete combustion** (plenty of O₂) → carbon dioxide and water only. A clean, hot blue flame.
- **Incomplete combustion** (limited O₂) → also produces **carbon monoxide (CO)** and **soot** (carbon). A smoky yellow flame.

<Callout type="warn" title="Why incomplete combustion is dangerous">
**Carbon monoxide** is a colourless, odourless, toxic gas — it binds to your blood's haemoglobin far more tightly than oxygen does, starving your body of oxygen. Faulty heaters and blocked flues that burn fuel without enough air are a real and silent hazard.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Combustion is exothermic" kind="explain">

Combustion always **releases** energy — it's strongly **exothermic**. The bonds in CO₂ and H₂O are more stable (lower energy) than those in the fuel and oxygen, and that energy difference comes out as heat and light. This is why fuels are stores of usable energy, the subject of a later world.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Combustion** is a fast reaction of a **fuel with oxygen**, releasing heat and light.
- **Complete** combustion of a hydrocarbon → **CO₂ + H₂O**; **incomplete** also makes toxic **CO** and **soot**.
- It is strongly **exothermic** — fuels store usable energy in their bonds.
- Carbon monoxide from incomplete burning is a colourless, odourless, deadly hazard.

</Step>
