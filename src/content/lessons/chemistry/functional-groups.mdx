<Step title="The reactive part of a molecule" kind="explain">

Most organic molecules are a fairly inert carbon backbone plus a small, reactive cluster of atoms called a **functional group**. The functional group is where the chemistry happens — it defines the molecule's family and its reactions.

This is a huge organising idea: learn a handful of functional groups and you can predict the behaviour of thousands of molecules, regardless of how big the carbon backbone is.

</Step>

<Step title="Swap the group" kind="interactive">

Keep the carbon backbone (R) and change the functional group. Each one defines a different family with different chemistry.

</Step>

<Step title="Families by functional group" kind="explain">

A few of the most important:

- **Alcohols** (–OH) — ethanol, the alcohol in drinks; also solvents and fuels.
- **Carboxylic acids** (–COOH) — acidic; acetic acid is vinegar.
- **Amines** (–NH₂) — basic; the building blocks of amino acids.
- **Aldehydes and ketones** (C=O) — reactive carbonyl compounds.
- **Esters** (–COO–) — sweet-smelling; fruit aromas and fats.

The carbon chain (the "R" group) barely matters for the reactions — the **functional group runs the show**.

</Step>

<Step title="Naming with functional groups" kind="explain">

Systematic names combine the chain stem with a suffix for the functional group: ethan**ol** (alcohol), ethano**ic acid** (carboxylic acid), propan**one** (ketone). The suffix tells you the family at a glance.

<Callout type="tip" title="Why this matters so much">
Functional groups are the alphabet of organic chemistry. A pharmacist designing a drug, a chemist reading a structure, a biologist studying metabolism — all reason in terms of which functional groups are present and how they'll react. Master the groups and organic chemistry becomes a language you can read.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- A **functional group** is the reactive cluster of atoms that defines an organic molecule's family and reactions.
- Key groups: **–OH** (alcohol), **–COOH** (carboxylic acid), **–NH₂** (amine), **C=O** (aldehyde/ketone), **–COO–** (ester).
- The carbon backbone is largely inert; the **functional group runs the chemistry**.
- Systematic **names** use a suffix per group — the alphabet of organic chemistry.
