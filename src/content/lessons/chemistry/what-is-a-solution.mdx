<Step title="Dissolved and evenly mixed" kind="explain">

A **solution** is a homogeneous mixture — one substance dissolved evenly in another, looking uniform throughout. Two words you'll use constantly:

- The **solute** is what gets dissolved (the salt, the sugar).
- The **solvent** is what does the dissolving (usually the larger amount — often water).

Salt water is salt (solute) dissolved in water (solvent). Because it's a *mixture*, the components keep their identity and can be separated again.

</Step>

<Step title="Watch something dissolve" kind="interactive">

Dissolving isn't disappearing. The solute particles spread out among the solvent and become too small and too spread to see.

<DissolveLab />

</Step>

<Step title="Solutions come in all states" kind="explain">

We usually picture a solid in a liquid, but solutions can be any combination of states:

- **Solid in liquid** — salt in water.
- **Gas in liquid** — carbon dioxide in fizzy drinks.
- **Liquid in liquid** — vinegar (acetic acid in water).
- **Gas in gas** — the air you breathe.
- **Solid in solid** — alloys like brass (zinc in copper).

If it's mixed evenly at the particle level, it's a solution.

</Step>

<Step title="Soluble, insoluble, miscible" kind="explain">

Some useful vocabulary:

- **Soluble** — it dissolves (sugar is soluble in water).
- **Insoluble** — it doesn't (sand is insoluble in water).
- **Miscible** — two liquids that mix in any proportion (alcohol and water).
- **Immiscible** — two liquids that refuse to mix (oil and water).

<Callout type="tip" title="Dissolving is physical, not chemical">
When salt dissolves, no new substance forms — it's a physical change. Evaporate the water and the salt comes back unchanged. That's exactly what makes a solution a mixture and not a compound.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- A **solution** is a homogeneous mixture: a **solute** dissolved evenly in a **solvent**.
- Solutions exist in **any combination of states** (solid/liquid/gas), including alloys and air.
- Vocabulary: **soluble/insoluble**, **miscible/immiscible**.
- Dissolving is a **physical** change — the solute can be recovered unchanged.

</Step>
