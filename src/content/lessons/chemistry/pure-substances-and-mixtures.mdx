<Step title="Pure, or mixed?" kind="explain">

Almost nothing around you is a single ingredient. Air is a blend of gases; seawater is water plus salts; steel is iron plus carbon. So chemists draw a first, big line through all matter:

- A **pure substance** has a fixed composition — every particle is the same kind throughout. Pure water is *only* H₂O molecules.
- A **mixture** contains two or more substances physically blended together, in *any* proportion, each keeping its own identity.

The crucial difference: a mixture can be **separated by physical means** (no chemistry needed), because its parts were never chemically joined.

</Step>

<Step title="Two kinds of pure substance" kind="explain">

A pure substance is either an **element** or a **compound**:

- An **element** is made of just one type of atom and can't be broken down chemically — copper, oxygen, gold. There are about 118 of them.
- A **compound** is two or more elements **chemically bonded** in a fixed ratio — water (H₂O), salt (NaCl), carbon dioxide (CO₂). Its properties are totally different from the elements that made it.

<Callout type="info" title="A compound is not a mixture">
Sodium is a metal that explodes in water; chlorine is a poison gas. Bond them and you get sodium chloride — table salt, which you eat. A compound has a fixed ratio and brand-new properties. A mixture of sodium and chlorine would just be metal bits and gas, still separable.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Two kinds of mixture" kind="explain">

Mixtures split by how evenly they're blended:

- **Homogeneous** — uniform throughout; you can't see the parts. Salt water, air, and brass look the same in every sample. (A homogeneous mixture is also called a **solution**.)
- **Heterogeneous** — non-uniform; you can see or pick out the parts. Sand in water, oil and vinegar, a salad.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Pure substances** have fixed composition; **mixtures** are physical blends in any ratio, separable by physical means.
- A pure substance is an **element** (one kind of atom) or a **compound** (elements chemically bonded in a fixed ratio).
- A compound has **new properties**, unlike the mixture of its ingredients.
- Mixtures are **homogeneous** (uniform / solutions) or **heterogeneous** (you can see the parts).

</Step>
