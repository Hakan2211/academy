<Lesson>

<Step title="One element, millions of compounds" kind="explain">

There are over a hundred elements, yet a single one — **carbon** — forms more compounds than all the others combined. The study of carbon compounds is **organic chemistry**, and it underlies fuels, plastics, medicines, and all of life.

Why carbon? Because of its bonding. With **four valence electrons**, carbon forms **four strong covalent bonds**, and — uniquely — it bonds readily **to itself**, chaining into endless structures.

</Step>

<Step title="Carbon's four bonds" kind="interactive">

Carbon's four bonds let it build chains, branches, and rings — the scaffolding of every organic molecule.

<CarbonBackbone />

</Step>

<Step title="Catenation: chaining to itself" kind="explain">

Carbon's superpower is **catenation** — its ability to bond to other carbon atoms over and over, forming long chains, branched trees, and closed rings. Few other elements do this to any real extent.

Combined with carbon's four bonds, this means an essentially **unlimited variety** of stable structures — straight chains, rings like benzene, and vast branching networks like DNA's backbone.

</Step>

<Step title="The backbone of life" kind="explain">

Every biological molecule — carbohydrates, fats, proteins, DNA — is built on a carbon skeleton. Carbon's versatility is precisely what lets life assemble the huge, intricate, stable molecules it needs.

<Callout type="info" title="Organic, then and now">
"Organic" once meant "from living things," because these compounds were first found in organisms. We now make millions in the lab, but the name stuck. Today *organic chemistry* simply means the chemistry of carbon compounds.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Organic chemistry** is the chemistry of **carbon** compounds — more than all other elements combined.
- Carbon forms **four covalent bonds** and bonds to itself (**catenation**) into chains, branches, and rings.
- This gives a near-**unlimited variety** of stable structures.
- Carbon skeletons are the backbone of **all life's molecules**.

</Step>
