<Step title="Pulling a mixture apart" kind="explain">

Because the parts of a mixture aren't chemically bonded, you can separate them using **physical differences** — no reaction required. The trick is to find a property in which the components differ:

- Different **particle size**? Filter them.
- Different **boiling point**? Boil one off.
- Different **attraction to a surface**? Let them race up paper.

Pick the right property and the mixture comes apart cleanly.

</Step>

<Step title="Matching method to mixture" kind="explain">

- **Filtration** separates an *insoluble solid* from a liquid using particle size — sand from water, tea leaves from tea.
- **Evaporation** recovers a *dissolved solid* by boiling the liquid away — salt from seawater.
- **Distillation** separates *liquids with different boiling points*, or gets pure liquid from a solution — fresh water from seawater, alcohol from a fermented mix.
- **Chromatography** separates *dissolved substances* (like dyes) by how strongly each sticks to the paper versus travels with the solvent.

<Callout type="tip" title="Distillation vs evaporation">
Evaporation throws the liquid away to keep the solid. Distillation *keeps the liquid* by catching and condensing its vapour. Same starting idea (boiling), opposite goal.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Why it works — and why it's still a mixture" kind="explain">

None of these methods involve a chemical reaction. You're never breaking bonds *within* a compound — only undoing the physical mixing. That's exactly why we call a mixture's components separable "by physical means," and it's the practical test that tells a mixture apart from a compound.

To split a *compound* like water into hydrogen and oxygen, you'd need a chemical change (electrolysis) — a job for a later world.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- Mixtures separate by **physical differences** — no reaction needed.
- **Filtration**: insoluble solid from liquid (size). **Evaporation**: dissolved solid recovered (boil liquid off).
- **Distillation**: pure liquid recovered by boiling then condensing. **Chromatography**: dissolved dyes by how they travel.
- Being separable by physical means is exactly what makes something a **mixture**, not a compound.

</Step>
