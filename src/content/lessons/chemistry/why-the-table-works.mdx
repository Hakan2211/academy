<Lesson>

<Step title="The shape is no accident" kind="explain">

Mendeleev built the table from *patterns in behaviour*, without knowing why they existed. The deep answer came decades later from **electron configuration**: the table's whole shape is a direct map of how electrons fill orbitals.

Every feature — the number of columns, the odd width of each block, where the metals sit — falls out of the filling order you met in the last world.

</Step>

<Step title="Blocks are sub-shells" kind="interactive">

Recolour the table by **block** and the structure jumps out. Each block is exactly the sub-shell that's being filled across it:

- **s-block** (2 columns) — filling an s sub-shell (2 electrons)
- **p-block** (6 columns) — filling a p sub-shell (6 electrons)
- **d-block** (10 columns) — the transition metals, filling d (10)
- **f-block** (14 columns) — the lanthanides and actinides, filling f (14)

<PeriodicTable mode="blocks" />

</Step>

<Step title="Count the columns" kind="explain">

Look at the widths: 2, then 6, then 10, then 14. Those are precisely the capacities of the s, p, d, and f sub-shells. The table is *wide where sub-shells are big* — its silhouette is a portrait of the orbitals.

A new **period** begins every time electrons start a new principal shell. That's why periods get longer further down: deeper shells contain more sub-shells to fill.

</Step>

<Step title="Configurations down a group" kind="interactive">

Now the punchline. Slide through the elements and compare members of the same group — say lithium (Z=3) and sodium (Z=11). Their *outer* configurations are identical: one s-electron each.

<ElectronConfig />

Same valence configuration → same chemistry. *That* is why a group is a family. The repeating pattern of valence electrons, recurring as each new shell fills, is the physical cause of the entire periodic law.

</Step>

<Step title="Trends, explained" kind="explain">

Configuration even explains the trends:

- **Across a period**, electrons enter the *same* shell while protons increase — stronger pull, smaller atoms, higher electronegativity.
- **Down a group**, each element adds a whole new shell — bigger atoms, electrons more shielded and easily lost.

<Callout type="info" title="From pattern to cause">
Mendeleev found *that* properties repeat; quantum mechanics revealed *why*. The periodic table is the meeting point of the two — a chart you can read off by hand, backed by the deepest physics of the atom. Next, we use it to predict how atoms **bond**.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- The table's shape **is** electron configuration: each **block** (s, p, d, f) is the sub-shell being filled.
- Block widths **2, 6, 10, 14** are the s/p/d/f capacities; a new **period** starts a new shell.
- Elements in a **group** share an outer configuration → same chemistry (the cause of the periodic law).
- Configuration also explains the **trends** across periods and down groups.

</Step>
