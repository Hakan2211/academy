<Step title="Unsaturated hydrocarbons" kind="explain">

Not all hydrocarbons are saturated. When carbons share **more than one** bond, the molecule is **unsaturated** — it holds *less* hydrogen than the equivalent alkane:

- **Alkenes** have a carbon–carbon **double bond** (C=C). Formula CₙH₂ₙ, ending **-ene**.
- **Alkynes** have a carbon–carbon **triple bond** (C≡C). Formula CₙH₂ₙ₋₂, ending **-yne**.

Ethene (C₂H₄) and ethyne (C₂H₂, used in welding torches) are the simplest examples.

</Step>

<Step title="The double bond is reactive" kind="explain">

That double bond is a region of concentrated electrons, and it makes alkenes far **more reactive** than alkanes. Their signature reaction is **addition**: the double bond opens up and atoms add across it.

- **Bromine water test:** orange bromine water is *decolourised* by an alkene (it adds across the C=C) but not by an alkane — a quick lab test for unsaturation.
- **Hydrogenation:** adding H₂ across the double bond turns an alkene into an alkane (this is how liquid oils are hardened into margarine).

</Step>

<Step title="Why unsaturation matters" kind="explain">

Unsaturation is everywhere it counts:

- **Ethene** is the most-produced organic chemical — the feedstock for polythene and countless plastics.
- **Unsaturated fats** (with C=C bonds) are liquid oils; **saturated fats** (no C=C) are solid — the same chemistry on your dinner plate.

<Callout type="info" title="Addition vs the next family">
The defining move of alkenes — *addition* across a double bond — is what makes them the starting point for polymers and so much of industry. Their reactivity is exactly why they're more useful than the inert alkanes.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Unsaturated** hydrocarbons have multiple bonds: **alkenes** (C=C, CₙH₂ₙ, -ene), **alkynes** (C≡C, CₙH₂ₙ₋₂, -yne).
- The double bond makes alkenes **reactive**, undergoing **addition** reactions.
- The **bromine water test** detects unsaturation (it's decolourised by alkenes).
- Ethene drives the plastics industry; C=C bonds distinguish unsaturated oils from saturated fats.

</Step>
