<Step title="The workhorses of the cell" kind="explain">

**Proteins** do almost everything in a living thing — they're enzymes, structural fibres, transporters, antibodies, and signals. They're polymers of **amino acids**, of which there are 20 kinds.

Each amino acid has the same core: a central carbon bonded to an **amine group** (–NH₂), a **carboxylic acid group** (–COOH), and a variable **side chain** (R) that makes each one different. Two functional groups you met in the organic world, on one molecule.

</Step>

<Step title="From chain to shape" kind="interactive">

Amino acids link into a chain that folds into a precise 3D shape — and that shape is the protein's function.

<ProteinFold />

</Step>

<Step title="The peptide bond" kind="explain">

Amino acids join by **condensation**: the –COOH of one reacts with the –NH₂ of the next, releasing water and forming a **peptide bond**. Many linked become a **polypeptide**, and a folded polypeptide is a protein.

This is just the acid + amine chemistry of the organic world, repeated to build a polymer — life's most versatile one.

</Step>

<Step title="Shape is everything — and fragile" kind="explain">

A protein's chain folds into a specific shape held by interactions between the side chains. That shape determines its job — an enzyme's pocket fits exactly one substrate, a fibre's coils give strength.

Heat it, or change the pH too far, and the delicate folding unravels — the protein **denatures** and stops working. (It's why a fried egg sets and never un-sets, and why fever is dangerous.)

<Callout type="info" title="Function follows folding">
The reason 20 simple amino acids can build millions of different proteins is the same reason carbon builds millions of compounds: combinatorial structure. The *sequence* sets the *fold*, and the fold sets the *function*. Structure determines properties — one last time.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Proteins** are the cell's workhorses — polymers of **20 amino acids**.
- Each amino acid has an **–NH₂**, a **–COOH**, and a variable **side chain (R)**.
- They join by **condensation** into **peptide bonds**; the chain **folds** into a precise shape.
- **Shape determines function**, and **denaturation** (heat/pH) destroys it — function follows folding.

</Step>
