<Step title="Sugars and starches" kind="explain">

**Carbohydrates** are the sugars and starches — life's quick energy and a lot of its structure. Their name hints at their make-up: "carbo-hydrate," carbon with water, roughly CₙH₂ₙOₙ. Glucose is C₆H₁₂O₆.

They build up in three sizes, from a single ring to vast chains.

</Step>

</Step>

<Step title="The three sizes" kind="explain">

- **Monosaccharides** — single sugar rings: **glucose** (blood sugar, cellular fuel), fructose, galactose.
- **Disaccharides** — two joined: **sucrose** (table sugar = glucose + fructose), lactose, maltose.
- **Polysaccharides** — long chains: **starch** and **glycogen** (energy storage), **cellulose** (plant cell walls and fibre).

The same glucose ring, linked in different ways, becomes either edible starch or indigestible cellulose — structure decides function yet again.

</Step>

<Step title="Made and broken with water" kind="explain">

Carbohydrates form by **condensation**: two sugars join with the loss of a water molecule (forming a *glycosidic* bond). They break by **hydrolysis**: adding water splits the bond again — which is exactly what digestion does to turn starch back into glucose.

<Callout type="tip" title="Energy on demand vs in store">
Glucose is the immediate fuel cells burn in respiration. The body chains spare glucose into **glycogen** (in liver and muscle) for short-term storage, and plants store it as **starch** — a compact, insoluble reserve that's hydrolysed back to glucose when needed.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Carbohydrates** (≈ CₙH₂ₙOₙ) are sugars and starches — fuel and structure.
- **Monosaccharides** (glucose) → **disaccharides** (sucrose) → **polysaccharides** (starch, glycogen, cellulose).
- They join by **condensation** (lose water) and break by **hydrolysis** (add water) — digestion.
- The same glucose, linked differently, gives digestible starch or structural cellulose.

</Step>
