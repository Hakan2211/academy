<Lesson>

<Step title="Reactions you can predict" kind="explain">

Organic chemistry can look like endless special cases — but underneath, a small set of **reaction types** governs almost everything. And which one happens is decided by the **functional group** present. Learn the patterns, and you can predict how a molecule will react.

The three master patterns:

- **Substitution** — one atom or group is swapped for another.
- **Addition** — atoms add across a double or triple bond (which disappears).
- **Elimination** — a small molecule is removed, *creating* a double bond.

</Step>

<Step title="The functional group decides" kind="interactive">

The reactive functional group is the key to what a molecule will do.

<FunctionalGroups />

Alkanes (no functional group) only do substitution; alkenes (C=C) do addition; alcohols can undergo elimination or oxidation. The group is the predictor.

</Step>

<Step title="Saturation and addition" kind="interactive">

Addition is the signature of unsaturated molecules — the double bond opens to accept new atoms.

<HydrocarbonViewer />

Hydrogenation (adding H₂) turns an alkene into an alkane; adding water makes an alcohol; adding many monomers makes a polymer. All are addition across the C=C.

</Step>

<Step title="A worked logic" kind="explain">

Put the pieces together to reason about a molecule:

- See an **alkane**? Inert except combustion and **substitution** (e.g., with halogens in light).
- See a **C=C (alkene)**? Expect **addition** — bromine, hydrogen, water, or polymerisation.
- See an **–OH (alcohol)**? It can be **oxidised** (to an acid) or undergo **elimination** (to an alkene).
- See a **–COOH + –OH**? Expect **esterification** (a condensation).

<Callout type="info" title="The reward of organic chemistry">
This is the payoff: instead of memorising reactions, you read the functional groups and predict the chemistry. From carbon's four bonds, through hydrocarbons, isomers, functional groups, and polymers, it all comes down to a few patterns driven by structure. Next, we apply this to the molecules of life itself.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- Three master organic reaction types: **substitution, addition, elimination** — chosen by the **functional group**.
- **Alkanes** → substitution/combustion; **alkenes (C=C)** → addition; **alcohols** → oxidation/elimination; acid + alcohol → ester.
- You **predict** reactions by reading the functional groups, not by memorising cases.
- Structure drives everything — the thread from carbon's bonds to the chemistry of life, next.

</Step>
