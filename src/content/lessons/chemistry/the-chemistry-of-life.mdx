<Step title="Everything, connected" kind="explain">

This is the summit of the whole island. Life is not a separate kind of science — it is **chemistry, organised**. Every idea you've built across fourteen worlds converges in a single living cell.

Let's trace the thread, from atoms to a heartbeat.

</Step>

<Step title="From atoms to molecules of life" kind="interactive">

It began with **atoms** and the **periodic table**, which set how elements **bond**. A handful of light elements — C, H, O, N, P, S — bond into the four families that build every living thing.

<BiomoleculeViewer />

Carbon's four bonds and its talent for chaining (the organic world) are *why* life's giant molecules can exist at all.

</Step>

<Step title="The chemistry that runs a cell" kind="explain">

Now watch the earlier worlds light up inside a cell:

- **Bonding & polarity** build proteins, DNA, and the phospholipid membrane (like dissolves like).
- **The mole & reactions** govern how much of each substance is made and used.
- **Solutions** — the cytoplasm is a watery solution where the chemistry happens.
- **Acids & bases** — buffers hold blood and cells at the exact pH enzymes need.
- **Thermochemistry** — respiration releases the energy stored in glucose's bonds.
- **Rates & equilibrium** — enzymes set reaction speeds; equilibria balance the cell.
- **Redox** — respiration is a controlled redox cascade, harvesting energy as ATP.

</Step>

<Step title="Enzymes: where it all happens" kind="interactive">

And tying the rate, catalysis, structure, and acid–base ideas together — the enzyme, life's catalyst.

<EnzymeLab />

Each of the thousands of enzymes in a cell is a folded protein whose shape (structure determines function) catalyses one step of metabolism.

</Step>

<Step title="Metabolism: chemistry as a living system" kind="explain">

**Metabolism** weaves it all into one self-sustaining network — catabolism releasing energy, anabolism building structure, ATP carrying energy between them, every step run by an enzyme and governed by the rules of thermodynamics, kinetics, and equilibrium you now understand.

A living cell is the most intricate chemical system known — and it breaks none of chemistry's laws. It simply runs them all at once, exquisitely organised.

<Callout type="info" title="The island, complete">
You started with "what is matter?" and arrived at the chemistry of life itself — bonding, energy, reactions, organic structures, and biochemistry, all one continuous story. Chemistry is the bridge between the physics of atoms and the biology of living things. You've now crossed it end to end.
</Callout>

</Step>
