<Step title="The skin of a solid" kind="explain">

**Surface area** is the total area of all the faces of a 3-D shape — the amount of "skin" you'd need to wrap it, or paint to cover it. The trick is to **unfold** the solid flat into its **net** and add up the face areas. It's an area, so it's measured in square units.

</Step>

<Step title="Unfold the box" kind="interactive">

A cuboid opens into six rectangles. Change the dimensions and watch the net.

<SurfaceArea />

</Step>

<Step title="A cuboid's six faces" kind="explain">

The six faces come in three matching pairs — top/bottom, front/back, left/right:

<Formula tex="\text{SA} = 2(lw + lh + wh)" block />

For a 4 × 3 × 2 box: 2(12 + 8 + 6) = 2 × 26 = 52 square units. Always work out each distinct face, double for its partner, and add.

</Step>

<Step title="Cylinders and other solids" kind="explain">

The same "unfold and add" idea works for any solid. A **cylinder** opens into two circles (the ends, 2 × πr²) and one rectangle (the curved side, which unrolls to a rectangle of width = the circumference 2πr):

<Formula tex="\text{SA of cylinder} = 2\pi r^2 + 2\pi r h" block />

Picture peeling the label off a tin — it's a rectangle as tall as the tin and as wide as the way around.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Surface area** = total area of all faces (square units).
- Unfold into a **net** and add the face areas.
- Cuboid: **2(lw + lh + wh)**; cube: 6×(edge)².
- Cylinder: two circles + an unrolled rectangle = **2πr² + 2πrh**.

</Step>
