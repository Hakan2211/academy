<Step title="Around versus inside" kind="explain">

Two different measurements of a flat shape, often confused:

- **Perimeter** — the total distance **around** the edge (a length, in cm, m, …).
- **Area** — the amount of surface **inside** (in *square* units, cm², m², …).

A bigger perimeter doesn't mean a bigger area, and vice versa — they measure different things.

</Step>

<Step title="Area formulas" kind="explain">

Area counts unit squares that fit inside:

<Formula tex="\text{rectangle} = b \times h, \quad \text{triangle} = \tfrac12 b h, \quad \text{parallelogram} = b \times h" block />

A triangle is half a rectangle (or parallelogram) of the same base and height — that's where the ½ comes from. **Perimeter** is always just the sum of all the side lengths, added the long way round.

</Step>

<Step title="Compound shapes" kind="explain">

Awkward shapes are handled by **splitting** them into rectangles and triangles, finding each area, and adding (or subtracting a cut-out). An L-shaped room = a big rectangle minus a corner, or two rectangles joined. Break the unfamiliar into the familiar — a strategy that runs through all of mensuration.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Perimeter** = distance around (length units); **area** = surface inside (square units).
- Rectangle b×h; triangle ½bh (half a rectangle); parallelogram b×h.
- **Compound shapes**: split into rectangles/triangles, then add or subtract.
- Bigger perimeter ≠ bigger area.

</Step>
