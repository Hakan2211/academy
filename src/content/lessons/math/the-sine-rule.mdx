<Step title="Trig without a right angle" kind="explain">

SOH-CAH-TOA only works in right triangles. For *any* triangle, the **sine rule** links each side to the angle opposite it:

<Formula tex="\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C}" block />

Each side divided by the sine of its opposite angle gives the **same** value, every time.

</Step>

<Step title="The ratio stays constant" kind="interactive">

Adjust the triangle and watch all three ratios stay equal.

<SineRule />

</Step>

<Step title="When to use it" kind="explain">

The sine rule needs a matched **side–opposite-angle pair**, plus one more piece. Use it when you know:

- two angles and any side (find the others), or
- two sides and an angle opposite one of them.

For example, with A = 40°, B = 60°, and side a = 8: then C = 80°, and b = a · sin B / sin A = 8 × sin 60° / sin 40° ≈ 10.8. Set up the rule, cross-multiply, solve.

</Step>

<Step title="The labelling convention" kind="explain">

Lower-case letters are **sides**, capitals are **angles**, and each side sits **opposite** its same-letter angle: side a faces angle A. Getting this pairing right is the whole game — the side and its opposite angle always travel together in the formula.

<Callout type="tip" title="Side up or angle up?">
To find a **side**, write the rule with sides on top (a/sin A = b/sin B). To find an **angle**, flip it (sin A / a = sin B / b) so the unknown sine is on top.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- The **sine rule** works in any triangle: a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C.
- Sides (lower-case) sit **opposite** their same-letter **angles** (capitals).
- Use it with a side–opposite-angle pair plus one more fact.
- Sides on top to find a side; sines on top to find an angle.

</Step>
