<Step title="Mathematics' most beautiful number" kind="explain">

One number has fascinated mathematicians, artists and architects for millennia: the **golden ratio**, φ (phi) ≈ **1.618**. It's the unique proportion where the ratio of the whole to the larger part equals the ratio of the larger part to the smaller — a self-similarity built into a single number.

<Formula tex="\varphi = \frac{1 + \sqrt{5}}{2} \approx 1.6180339\ldots" block />

</Step>

<Step title="Born from Fibonacci" kind="interactive">

You met it back in the Sequences world: divide consecutive Fibonacci numbers and you spiral straight into φ.

<FibonacciSpiral />

</Step>

<Step title="Its strange self-referential nature" kind="explain">

φ has a property no other number shares so cleanly — squaring it is the same as adding one:

<Formula tex="\varphi^2 = \varphi + 1, \qquad \frac{1}{\varphi} = \varphi - 1" block />

It's also, in a precise sense, the **"most irrational" number** — the very hardest to approximate by any fraction. That's not a flaw but a feature: it's exactly *why* plants space their leaves and seeds by the golden angle (≈137.5°), so no two ever overlap and each catches the most light. Sunflowers and pinecones grow by φ for pure efficiency.

</Step>

<Step title="Beauty, myth, and honesty" kind="explain">

φ genuinely appears in nature's growth and in some art and architecture — the **golden rectangle** (sides in ratio φ) does feel pleasingly balanced. But beware the myths: many claims that the Parthenon, the Mona Lisa, or credit cards were "designed around φ" are exaggerated or invented after the fact. Part of mathematical maturity is loving a beautiful number **and** demanding evidence for claims about it.

<Callout type="info" title="Where it's truly remarkable">
The honest wonder isn't the dubious art claims — it's that a number defined by a fraction-and-a-square-root falls out of rabbit populations, sunflower spirals, and the geometry of the pentagon alike. That recurrence across unrelated places is the real magic.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- The **golden ratio φ ≈ 1.618** = (1 + √5)/2, the proportion where whole : large = large : small.
- It emerges from the **Fibonacci** sequence and satisfies **φ² = φ + 1**.
- As the "**most irrational**" number, it's why plants use the golden angle for efficient growth.
- Real in nature and pleasing in design — but treat sensational art/architecture claims with healthy skepticism.

</Step>
