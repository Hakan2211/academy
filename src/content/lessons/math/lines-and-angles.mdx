<Step title="Measuring turn" kind="explain">

An **angle** measures the amount of turn between two lines meeting at a point, in **degrees** (°). A full turn is 360°, a quarter-turn is 90° (a **right angle**). Angles are the alphabet of geometry — every shape is built from them.

</Step>

<Step title="Naming angles by size" kind="explain">

- **Acute:** less than 90°
- **Right:** exactly 90° (marked with a little square)
- **Obtuse:** between 90° and 180°
- **Straight:** 180° (a straight line)
- **Reflex:** more than 180°

</Step>

<Step title="Angle facts that always hold" kind="explain">

A few rules let you find unknown angles without measuring:

- Angles on a **straight line** add to **180°**.
- Angles around a **point** add to **360°**.
- **Vertically opposite** angles (across an X-crossing) are **equal**.

So if a straight line is split into 130° and an unknown, the unknown is 180 − 130 = **50°**. These facts chain together to crack surprisingly complex diagrams.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- An **angle** measures turn in degrees; a right angle is 90°, a full turn 360°.
- Classify by size: **acute, right, obtuse, straight, reflex**.
- Angles on a **line** sum to 180°; around a **point** to 360°; **vertically opposite** angles are equal.
- These facts find unknown angles without a protractor.

</Step>
