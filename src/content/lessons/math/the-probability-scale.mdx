<Step title="Measuring chance" kind="explain">

**Probability** measures how likely something is, on a scale from **0** to **1**. Zero means impossible; one means certain; one-half means an even chance. Every probability — for any event in the universe — lands somewhere on this single line.

</Step>

</Step>

<Step title="Three ways to write it" kind="explain">

A probability can be a fraction, a decimal, or a percentage — all the same number:

<Formula tex="P = \tfrac{1}{2} = 0.5 = 50\%" block />

"Unlikely" sits below ½, "likely" above. A weather forecast of "70% chance of rain" is just P = 0.7 on this scale.

</Step>

<Step title="The complement" kind="explain">

Because something either happens or it doesn't, the probability of an event and the probability of it **not** happening must add to 1:

<Formula tex="P(\text{not } A) = 1 - P(A)" block />

If the chance of rain is 0.7, the chance of no rain is 1 − 0.7 = 0.3. This "complement" trick is often the easiest route — sometimes it's far simpler to work out the opposite and subtract.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Probability** runs from **0** (impossible) to **1** (certain); ½ is even chance.
- It can be written as a **fraction, decimal, or percentage**.
- The **complement**: P(not A) = **1 − P(A)**.
- Nothing has probability below 0 or above 1.

</Step>
