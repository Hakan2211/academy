<Step title="One principle behind it all" kind="explain">

Every technique in this world — linear equations, brackets, fractions, simultaneous pairs, inequalities — is the *same* idea wearing different clothes: **keep the balance while isolating the unknown.** Master that one principle and you can solve things you've never been taught, because you're not following recipes — you understand why they work.

</Step>

<Step title="Balance is the whole game" kind="interactive">

Every legal move keeps both sides equal. The art is choosing moves that simplify.

<BalanceScale />

</Step>

<Step title="How many solutions?" kind="explain">

Not every equation has exactly one answer. Watch what the unknown does:

- **One solution:** 2x = 8 → x = 4. The normal case.
- **No solution:** x + 1 = x + 2. Subtract x → 1 = 2, false. The lines are parallel and never meet.
- **Infinitely many:** 2(x + 1) = 2x + 2. Subtract 2x → 2 = 2, always true. Both sides are the *same* equation; every x works.

When the variable cancels out, the equation is telling you something — read the leftover statement.

</Step>

<Step title="Strategy and self-checking" kind="explain">

The habits of a confident solver:

- **Simplify before you solve** — expand brackets, clear fractions, collect terms.
- **Plan your moves** — clear constants, then coefficients; for two unknowns, eliminate or substitute.
- **Always check** by substituting back. An equation grades its own answer.
- **Stay alert to traps** — the negative-flip in inequalities, and the "variable vanished" cases above.

<Callout type="info" title="Why this matters for everything ahead">
Solving is how mathematics answers questions. Find where a graph crosses an axis, when a projectile lands, what interest rate doubles your money — each is an equation waiting to be solved. The Quadratics world is next, where the unknown appears squared and a brand-new tool is needed.
</Callout>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- All equation-solving is one idea: **keep the balance, isolate the unknown**.
- An equation can have **one**, **no**, or **infinitely many** solutions — watch when the variable cancels.
- **Simplify first**, plan your moves, and **always check**.
- Solving is the engine of applied maths — and the gateway to quadratics next.
