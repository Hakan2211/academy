<Step title="Shrinking the world to fit a page" kind="explain">

A map, a floor plan, a model car — each is a faithful shrink of something real. The **scale** tells you the shrink factor. A scale of **1 : 50** means every 1 cm on the model is 50 cm in real life. The shape is preserved exactly; only the size changes.

</Step>

<Step title="Converting with a scale" kind="explain">

A scale is just a ratio, used in two directions:

- **model → real:** multiply by the scale factor.
- **real → model:** divide by it.

On a 1 : 25 000 map, 4 cm represents 4 × 25 000 = 100 000 cm = **1 km**. Watch the units: the answer comes out in the small unit (cm), so convert at the end.

<Formula tex="\text{real distance} = \text{map distance} \times \text{scale factor}" block />

</Step>

<Step title="Areas scale by the square" kind="explain">

Here's the trap. If lengths scale by 50, **areas scale by 50²= 2500**, because area has two dimensions. A 1 : 50 model with 4 cm² of floor represents 4 × 2500 = 10 000 cm² of real floor — not 4 × 50. (And volumes scale by the **cube**.) You'll prove exactly why in the Measurement world.

<Callout type="warn" title="Don't scale area by the length factor">
Lengths × k, areas × k², volumes × k³. Forgetting the square or cube is the most common scaling error there is.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- A **scale** 1 : n means 1 unit on the model is n units in reality.
- **model → real**: × n; **real → model**: ÷ n; mind the units.
- **Lengths × k, areas × k², volumes × k³** — scaling is dimensional.

</Step>
