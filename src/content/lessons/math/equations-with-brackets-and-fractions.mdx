<Lesson>

<Step title="Tidy first, then solve" kind="explain">

Messier equations hide a plain linear equation underneath. The strategy is to **clear the clutter** — expand brackets and remove fractions — until you're back to the familiar "undo in reverse" you already know.

</Step>

</Step>

<Step title="Clearing brackets" kind="explain">

Expand any brackets first, then proceed as normal:

<Formula tex="3(x - 2) = 12 \;\to\; 3x - 6 = 12 \;\to\; 3x = 18 \;\to\; x = 6" block />

You *could* divide by 3 first here, but expanding always works, especially when brackets sit on both sides.

</Step>

<Step title="Clearing fractions" kind="explain">

Fractions vanish if you **multiply every term by the denominator** (or the LCM of several):

<Formula tex="\frac{x}{3} + 2 = 5 \;\xrightarrow{\times 3}\; x + 6 = 15 \;\to\; x = 9" block />

With two fractions, multiply through by the **LCM** of the denominators so all of them clear at once. Multiply *every* term — including the ones without a fraction.

<Callout type="warn" title="Multiply the whole side">
When you multiply to clear a fraction, every term gets multiplied — not just the fraction. Forgetting the "+2" above (→ 6) is the most common error here.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Expand brackets** and **clear fractions** before solving.
- Clear a fraction by **multiplying every term** by the denominator (or the LCM).
- Then it's an ordinary linear equation — undo in reverse.
- Multiply the **whole side**, not just the fractional term.

</Step>
