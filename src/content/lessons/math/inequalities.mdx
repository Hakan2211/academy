<Step title="When 'equals' becomes 'at least'" kind="explain">

Not everything is exact. "You must be **at least** 18", "the budget is **under** £50", "speed **up to** 30 mph" — these are **inequalities**. Instead of one answer, they describe a whole **range** of values.

- **>** greater than &nbsp;&nbsp; **≥** greater than or equal to
- **&lt;** less than &nbsp;&nbsp; **≤** less than or equal to

</Step>

<Step title="A range on the number line" kind="interactive">

The circle marks the boundary — open if it's excluded (&lt;, >), filled if included (≤, ≥) — and the shaded ray is every value that works.

<InequalityLine />

</Step>

<Step title="Solve them just like equations" kind="explain">

Inequalities are solved with the **same** balanced moves as equations:

<Formula tex="3x + 2 < 14 \;\to\; 3x < 12 \;\to\; x < 4" block />

So the solution is *every* number below 4, not a single value.

</Step>

<Step title="The one crucial twist" kind="explain">

There is exactly one new rule, and it's vital: **multiplying or dividing by a negative flips the inequality sign.**

<Formula tex="-2x < 6 \;\xrightarrow{\div(-2)}\; x > -3" block />

Why? On the number line, multiplying by a negative reflects everything through zero, reversing the order — what was smaller becomes larger. (Adding or subtracting never flips the sign; only ×/÷ by a negative does.)

<Callout type="warn" title="Flip on negative ×/÷">
< becomes >, ≤ becomes ≥, whenever you multiply or divide both sides by a negative number. Forgetting this reverses your whole answer.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- An **inequality** describes a **range** of values, not a single answer.
- Solve with the **same** balanced steps as equations.
- **Flip the sign** when multiplying or dividing by a **negative**.
- On the line: open circle = excluded, filled = included; the ray is the solution set.

</Step>
