<Step title="More of one, less of the other" kind="explain">

Sometimes quantities pull in **opposite** directions. Twice as many builders finish a wall in **half** the time. Travel twice as fast and the journey takes **half** as long. This is **inverse proportion**: as one goes up, the other comes down — but their **product** stays fixed.

</Step>

<Step title="The rule" kind="explain">

If y is inversely proportional to x (written y ∝ 1/x), then

<Formula tex="y = \frac{k}{x}, \qquad xy = k \text{ (constant)}" block />

Find k by multiplying any known pair. If 6 workers take 10 days, then k = 6 × 10 = 60 "worker-days." So 4 workers take 60 ÷ 4 = 15 days.

</Step>

<Step title="Line versus curve" kind="explain">

This is the visual giveaway:

- **Direct** proportion (y = kx) → a **straight line** through the origin.
- **Inverse** proportion (y = k/x) → a **curve** that swoops down, never touching either axis (you can't finish the job with zero workers, or in zero time).

Recognising which one you're facing is half the battle.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Inverse proportion**: one up, the other down, with a **constant product**.
- **y = k/x**, so **xy = k**; find k by multiplying a known pair.
- Graph is a **curve** (a reciprocal), never touching the axes.
- Direct = line through origin; inverse = downward curve.

</Step>
