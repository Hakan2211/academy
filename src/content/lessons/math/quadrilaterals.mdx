<Step title="Four-sided shapes" kind="explain">

A **quadrilateral** is any four-sided polygon. Its four interior angles always add to **360°** — because a diagonal splits it into two triangles, each contributing 180°.

<Formula tex="2 \times 180° = 360°" block />

</Step>

<Step title="The family of quadrilaterals" kind="explain">

They form a hierarchy, each a special case of a broader type:

- **Square** — 4 equal sides, 4 right angles (the most special).
- **Rectangle** — 4 right angles, opposite sides equal.
- **Rhombus** — 4 equal sides, opposite angles equal.
- **Parallelogram** — both pairs of opposite sides parallel and equal.
- **Trapezium** — exactly one pair of parallel sides.
- **Kite** — two pairs of adjacent equal sides.

A square is a special rectangle *and* a special rhombus — properties inherit downward.

</Step>

<Step title="Using the properties" kind="explain">

Knowing a shape's properties lets you find missing angles and sides. In a **parallelogram**, opposite angles are equal and co-interior angles (adjacent) sum to 180°. In any quadrilateral, if you know three angles, the fourth is 360° minus their sum. The diagonals carry information too — in a rhombus they cross at right angles; in a rectangle they're equal.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- A **quadrilateral**'s angles sum to **360°** (two triangles).
- The family: **square, rectangle, rhombus, parallelogram, trapezium, kite** — special cases inherit properties.
- Use known properties (parallel sides, equal angles, diagonals) to find unknowns.

</Step>
