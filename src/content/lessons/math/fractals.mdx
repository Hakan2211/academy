<Step title="Infinite detail from a simple rule" kind="explain">

A **fractal** is a shape that contains smaller copies of itself, at every scale, forever. Zoom in and you see the same structure again and again. They arise from the simplest of recipes — "do this, then repeat" — yet produce endless, intricate detail. Fractals are where the infinite becomes visible.

</Step>

<Step title="Self-similarity and fractional dimension" kind="explain">

The defining feature is **self-similarity**: any piece, magnified, looks like the whole. This leads to a wild idea — fractals have **fractional dimension**. A line is 1-D, a square 2-D, but the Sierpinski triangle's dimension is:

<Formula tex="d = \frac{\log 3}{\log 2} \approx 1.585" block />

It's "more than a line but less than a filled area" — too holey to be 2-D, too dense to be 1-D. Dimension stops being a whole number.

</Step>

<Step title="Nature is fractal" kind="explain">

Once you see fractals, they're everywhere — because growth and branching naturally repeat at every scale:

- **Coastlines** look equally jagged from space or up close (the "coastline paradox" — their length depends on your ruler).
- **Trees, ferns, lungs, blood vessels, river networks, lightning** all branch fractally.
- **Clouds, mountains and snowflakes** are statistically self-similar.

The Mandelbrot set — generated by endlessly iterating z → z² + c — is perhaps the most famous, an infinitely detailed boundary from a one-line rule.

<Callout type="info" title="Benoît Mandelbrot">
Mandelbrot coined "fractal" in 1975 and showed this rough, repeating geometry describes the real world far better than the smooth lines of classical geometry. "Clouds are not spheres, mountains are not cones," he wrote — nature is fractal.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- A **fractal** repeats its structure at **every scale** — self-similarity from a simple repeated rule.
- They can have **fractional dimension** (Sierpinski ≈ 1.585).
- **Nature is fractal**: coastlines, trees, lungs, clouds, lightning.
- The **Mandelbrot set** shows infinite detail from z → z² + c.
