<Step title="Shorthand for repeated multiplication" kind="explain">

Just as multiplication is repeated addition, a **power** is repeated multiplication:

<Formula tex="2^5 = 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 2 = 32" block />

The **base** (2) is what you multiply; the **index** or **exponent** (5) counts how many copies. We say "2 to the power 5." Powers let us write enormous numbers compactly — and they're the natural language of growth.

</Step>

<Step title="Squares and cubes" kind="explain">

Two powers have their own names from geometry:

- **n²** ("n squared") is the area of an n × n square.
- **n³** ("n cubed") is the volume of an n × n × n cube.

The square numbers 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, … are exactly the dots you can arrange in a perfect square.

</Step>

</Step>

<Step title="Roots undo powers" kind="explain">

A **square root** answers "what number, squared, gives this?" Since 7² = 49, we have √49 = 7. A **cube root** undoes cubing: ∛27 = 3.

Most roots aren't whole. √49 is exactly 7, but √50 ≈ 7.07… — it sits just past a perfect square. Roots that never come out exactly (like √2) are the irrational numbers from World 1, and you'll tame them as **surds** later.

<Callout type="info" title="Every positive number has two square roots">
Both 5² and (−5)² equal 25, so 25 has square roots +5 and −5. The symbol √ means just the positive one, called the principal root.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- A **power** bᵉ multiplies the base b by itself e times.
- **n²** is a square's area, **n³** a cube's volume; square numbers are 1, 4, 9, 16, …
- **Roots** undo powers: √ undoes squaring, ∛ undoes cubing.
- Non-perfect roots (√2, √50) are irrational — they never terminate.

</Step>
