<Lesson>

<Step title="Ratios simplify like fractions" kind="explain">

Divide every part of a ratio by a common factor and it stays the same ratio in simpler form:

<Formula tex="12 : 18 \;\xrightarrow{\div 6}\; 2 : 3" block />

Divide by the **HCF** of the parts to reach the simplest form in one step — exactly the skill from the Factors & Primes world.

</Step>

<Step title="Share a total in a given ratio" kind="interactive">

The key move: count the **total number of parts**, find what **one part** is worth, then give each share its number of parts.

<RatioMixer />

</Step>

<Step title="The three-step share" kind="explain">

Split £40 between two people in the ratio 3 : 5:

1. Total parts: 3 + 5 = **8**.
2. One part: £40 ÷ 8 = **£5**.
3. Shares: 3 × £5 = **£15** and 5 × £5 = **£25**.

<Formula tex="15 + 25 = 40 \checkmark" block />

Always check the shares add back to the total — it catches almost every slip.

</Step>

<Step title="Ratios as 1 : n" kind="explain">

Dividing both parts so the first becomes 1 gives the **unitary ratio** 1 : n, which is perfect for comparison. 5 : 8 becomes 1 : 1.6 — "for every 1 of the first, 1.6 of the second." This is how map scales and gear ratios are written.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Simplify** a ratio by dividing every part by their HCF.
- **Share** a total: add the parts, find one part, multiply each share.
- **Check** the shares sum back to the total.
- The **1 : n** form is ideal for comparing and for scales.

</Step>
