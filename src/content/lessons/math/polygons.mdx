<Step title="Many-sided shapes" kind="explain">

A **polygon** is a closed shape made of straight sides: triangle (3), quadrilateral (4), pentagon (5), hexagon (6), and on. A **regular** polygon has all sides and all angles equal. Two angle rules govern every one of them.

</Step>

<Step title="Interior angles: split into triangles" kind="explain">

Any n-sided polygon can be cut from one vertex into **(n − 2)** triangles, each 180°. So the interior angles sum to:

<Formula tex="\text{interior sum} = (n - 2) \times 180°" block />

A pentagon (n = 5): 3 × 180 = 540°. For a *regular* polygon, each interior angle is this sum ÷ n.

</Step>

<Step title="Exterior angles: always 360°" kind="explain">

Walk all the way around a polygon and you turn through one full circle. So the **exterior angles always sum to 360°**, no matter how many sides:

<Formula tex="\text{each exterior angle (regular)} = \frac{360°}{n}" block />

A regular hexagon turns 360 ÷ 6 = 60° at each corner. Interior and exterior angles at each vertex sit on a straight line, so they add to 180° — giving you two ways to find either one.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- A **polygon** is a closed straight-sided shape; **regular** = all sides/angles equal.
- **Interior sum = (n − 2) × 180°** (cut into n − 2 triangles).
- **Exterior angles always sum to 360°**; each regular one is 360°/n.
- Interior + exterior at a vertex = 180°.

</Step>
