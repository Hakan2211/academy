<Lesson>

<Step title="Numbers need units" kind="explain">

"It's 5 long" is meaningless — 5 *what*? A measurement is a **number with a unit**. The **metric system** makes conversions painless because every unit is related by **powers of ten**: 10 mm = 1 cm, 100 cm = 1 m, 1000 m = 1 km.

</Step>

<Step title="Climb the metric staircase" kind="interactive">

Convert a length between units — each step is just a multiply or divide by 10, 100 or 1000.

<UnitConverter />

</Step>

<Step title="Convert by multiplying or dividing" kind="explain">

Going to a **smaller** unit, the number gets **bigger** (more of them fit), so you **multiply**. Going to a **bigger** unit, you **divide**:

<Formula tex="3.2 \text{ m} = 3.2 \times 100 = 320 \text{ cm}" block />

The same logic covers mass (mg, g, kg, tonnes) and capacity (ml, l) — all powered by ten.

</Step>

<Step title="The trap: area and volume" kind="explain">

Lengths convert by the factor; **areas and volumes do not**. Because area is length × length, its factor is **squared**; volume is length³, so **cubed**:

<Formula tex="1 \text{ m}^2 = 100^2 = 10\,000 \text{ cm}^2, \qquad 1 \text{ m}^3 = 100^3 = 1\,000\,000 \text{ cm}^3" block />

<Callout type="warn" title="Don't convert area with the length factor">
1 m² is NOT 100 cm² — picture a 1 m × 1 m square as 100 cm × 100 cm = 10 000 cm². Square the conversion factor for area, cube it for volume.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- A measurement is a **number + unit**; metric units relate by **powers of ten**.
- To a smaller unit → **multiply**; to a bigger unit → **divide**.
- **Areas** use the **squared** factor, **volumes** the **cubed** factor.
- 1 m² = 10 000 cm²; 1 m³ = 1 000 000 cm³.

</Step>
