<Lesson>

<Step title="Three costumes, one number" kind="explain">

½, 0.5 and 50% all name the *same* amount — they're just three notations for one quantity. Fractions are exact and great for reasoning; decimals slot into place value and calculators; percentages compare things out of 100. Fluent maths means switching costume at will.

</Step>

<Step title="Fraction → decimal" kind="explain">

A fraction is a division, so divide to get its decimal:

<Formula tex="\frac{3}{8} = 3 \div 8 = 0.375" block />

Some divide exactly (they **terminate**), others repeat forever — a story we'll finish in this world's deep dive.

</Step>

<PercentGrid />

</Step>

<Step title="The conversion map" kind="explain">

- **Decimal → percent:** × 100 (move the point two places). 0.375 → 37.5%.
- **Percent → decimal:** ÷ 100. 37.5% → 0.375.
- **Decimal → fraction:** read the place value. 0.375 = 375/1000 = 3/8.
- **Fraction → decimal:** divide top by bottom.

<Callout type="tip" title="Worth memorising">
A handful of conversions come up constantly: ½ = 0.5 = 50%, ¼ = 0.25 = 25%, ⅕ = 0.2 = 20%, ⅓ ≈ 0.333 = 33⅓%. Knowing these by heart speeds up everything.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- Fractions, decimals and percents are **three notations for one number**.
- **Fraction → decimal**: divide. **Decimal ↔ percent**: × or ÷ 100.
- **Decimal → fraction**: read its place value, then simplify.
- Convert everything to one form to **compare** confidently.

</Step>
