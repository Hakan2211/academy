<Step title="Below zero" kind="explain">

What's 3 − 5? If numbers stopped at zero, this question would have no answer. So the number line extends **left of zero** into the **negative numbers**.

You already use them: temperatures below freezing (−4°C), money owed (a balance of −£20), floors below ground level. Together the negatives, zero, and the positive whole numbers make up the **integers**: … −3, −2, −1, 0, 1, 2, 3 …

</Step>

<Step title="Adding and subtracting on the line" kind="interactive">

Every addition is a step **right**; every subtraction is a step **left**. Set a starting point and a change, and watch the jump.

<NumberLine min={-10} max={10} jumps />

</Step>

<Step title="The two minus signs" kind="explain">

A minus sign does two different jobs, and telling them apart is the whole game:

- A **sign**: −5 is a *number*, five to the left of zero.
- An **operation**: 8 − 5 is an *instruction*, "subtract 5."

Adding a negative moves you left, just like subtracting:

<Formula tex="8 + (-5) = 8 - 5 = 3" block />

And subtracting a negative reverses a reversal — you move **right**:

<Formula tex="8 - (-5) = 8 + 5 = 13" block />

</Step>

<Step title="Multiplying with signs" kind="explain">

When you multiply or divide, only the **signs** decide the result's sign:

- same signs → **positive**:  (−4) × (−3) = +12
- different signs → **negative**:  (−4) × 3 = −12

A neat way to see "negative times negative is positive": multiplying by −1 flips a number to the other side of zero. Flip twice and you're back where you started.

<Callout type="info" title="Why two flips return home">
−1 × 5 = −5 (flipped once). −1 × (−5) = +5 (flipped back). Two negatives undo each other.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Integers** are the whole numbers extended left of zero: … −2, −1, 0, 1, 2 …
- **Adding** moves right on the line; **subtracting** moves left.
- Adding a negative = subtracting;  subtracting a negative = adding.
- For × and ÷: **same signs → positive, different signs → negative**.

</Step>
