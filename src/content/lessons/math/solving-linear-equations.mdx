<Step title="Undo, in reverse" kind="explain">

A **linear** equation has the unknown to the first power only — no x², no √x. To solve one, you **undo** the operations wrapped around x, in reverse order, using inverse operations (+ ↔ −, × ↔ ÷). Strip them off until x stands alone.

</Step>

<Step title="Step through a solve" kind="interactive">

Each move is a balanced operation; the equation simplifies toward x = something.

<EquationSolver />

</Step>

<Step title="A worked order of attack" kind="explain">

For 2x + 3 = 11: the x has been multiplied by 2, *then* had 3 added. Undo last-first — subtract 3, then divide by 2:

<Formula tex="2x + 3 = 11 \;\to\; 2x = 8 \;\to\; x = 4" block />

General plan: **+/− first to clear constants, then ×/÷ to clear the coefficient.**

</Step>

<Step title="Unknown on both sides" kind="explain">

When x appears on both sides, gather the x-terms onto one side and the numbers onto the other:

<Formula tex="5x - 4 = 2x + 8 \;\to\; 3x - 4 = 8 \;\to\; 3x = 12 \;\to\; x = 4" block />

Subtract 2x from both sides first (move the smaller x-term to keep things positive), then finish as usual.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Linear** = unknown to the first power only.
- **Undo** operations in reverse, with inverses: clear constants (+/−), then the coefficient (×/÷).
- Unknown on **both sides**: gather x-terms one side, numbers the other.
- Always **check** by substituting back.

</Step>
