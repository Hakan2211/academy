<Step title="Hidden order in the circle" kind="explain">

The circle hides a set of surprising, exact angle relationships — the **circle theorems**. They look like coincidences until you prove them, and they're a perfect showcase of how geometry builds certainty from a few starting facts. This is the summit of the geometry world.

<Step title="Thales' theorem: 90° in a semicircle" kind="explain">

Pick any point on a circle and join it to the two ends of a **diameter**. The angle there is **always 90°** — a right angle — wherever the point sits.

<Formula tex="\text{angle in a semicircle} = 90°" block />

The ancient Greek Thales is said to have proved it 2,600 years ago. It means you can draw a right angle with nothing but a circle and a diameter.

</Step>

<Step title="The angle at the centre is double" kind="explain">

For the same arc, the angle at the **centre** is exactly **twice** the angle at the **circumference**:

<Formula tex="\angle\text{(centre)} = 2 \times \angle\text{(circumference)}" block />

Thales' theorem is actually a special case: when the arc is a semicircle, the centre angle is 180°, so the circumference angle is 90°. And every angle drawn on the **same arc** is equal (they're all half the same central angle). Other gems follow: a **cyclic quadrilateral** (corners on a circle) has opposite angles summing to 180°.

</Step>

<Step title="Why proof is the point" kind="explain">

You could measure a hundred circles and *believe* these facts — but geometry **proves** them, so they hold for every circle that could ever exist. The angle-at-the-centre theorem follows in a few lines from the isosceles triangles formed by radii and the exterior-angle rule from the triangles world. This is the spirit of Euclid: from a handful of self-evident axioms, an entire universe of certain truths unfolds by pure logic.

<Callout type="info" title="From Euclid to today">
Euclid's *Elements* (≈300 BC) built all of geometry from five axioms and was the model of rigorous reasoning for two thousand years. The circle theorems are a small, beautiful corner of that edifice — and proof, not measurement, is what makes mathematics eternal.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Thales:** the angle in a semicircle is always **90°**.
- **Angle at centre = 2 × angle at circumference** (same arc); same-arc angles are equal.
- **Cyclic quadrilateral:** opposite angles sum to **180°**.
- Geometry **proves** these for every circle — proof, not measurement, is what endures.

</Step>
