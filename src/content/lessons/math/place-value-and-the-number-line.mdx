<Lesson>

<Step title="Every number has a place" kind="explain">

Mathematics begins with a single, powerful idea: a straight line where every number has its own spot. Smaller numbers sit to the **left**, larger ones to the **right**, and the gaps between them are perfectly even.

This is the **number line** — the stage on which all of arithmetic plays out. Counting up means stepping right; counting down means stepping left.

</Step>

<Step title="Find a number's place" kind="interactive">

Drag the marker and watch where each number lives. Notice the spacing never changes — the step from 3 to 4 is exactly the step from 7 to 8.

<NumberLine min={0} max={10} />

</Step>

<Step title="The value of a digit depends on where it sits" kind="explain">

Here is the trick that lets ten symbols (0–9) write *any* number: **place value**. The digit 3 means something different in 3, 30, and 300 — each column to the left is worth **ten times** the one on its right.

So 3204 isn't four random digits. It's:

<Formula tex="3000 + 200 + 0 + 4" block />

The 0 is doing real work — it holds the tens column empty so the 2 stays in the hundreds.

</Step>

<Step title="Build a number" kind="interactive">

Change each column and watch the total — and its expanded form — update. Then try the **× 10** button: every digit slides one place to the left.

<PlaceValue />

</Step>

<Step title="Decimals carry on to the right" kind="explain">

The columns don't stop at the ones. Past the decimal point they keep dividing by ten: **tenths**, then **hundredths**, then thousandths.

So 4.56 means four ones, five tenths, and six hundredths. The decimal point is just the marker that says "the ones column ends here." Place value runs in both directions, forever.

<Callout type="tip" title="Reading decimals">
0.5 and 0.50 are the *same number* — adding zeros on the far right of a decimal changes nothing, because those columns are simply empty.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- The **number line** orders every number: left = smaller, right = larger, even spacing throughout.
- **Place value** gives each digit a worth ten times the column to its right.
- A **0** is a placeholder that keeps the other digits in their proper columns.
- Decimals extend place value rightward into **tenths, hundredths, …**.

</Step>
