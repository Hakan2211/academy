<Step title="Putting the ratios to work" kind="explain">

The real power of SOH-CAH-TOA is solving triangles: given an angle and a side, find a missing side; given two sides, find a missing angle. The whole skill is **picking the right ratio** for the sides you have.

<Step title="Finding a missing side" kind="explain">

Label the sides relative to the known angle, then choose the ratio that uses your known and unknown sides. To find the opposite side from the hypotenuse:

<Formula tex="\sin\theta = \frac{O}{H} \;\Rightarrow\; O = H \sin\theta" block />

A 10 m ladder at 70° to the ground reaches 10 × sin 70° ≈ 9.4 m up the wall. Pick the ratio, rearrange, compute.

</Step>

<Step title="Finding a missing angle" kind="explain">

Know two sides? Use the **inverse** trig functions (sin⁻¹, cos⁻¹, tan⁻¹) to recover the angle:

<Formula tex="\tan\theta = \frac{O}{A} \;\Rightarrow\; \theta = \tan^{-1}\!\left(\frac{O}{A}\right)" block />

A ramp rising 2 m over 5 m horizontal: θ = tan⁻¹(2/5) ≈ 21.8°. The inverse function answers "which angle has this ratio?"

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Side:** pick the ratio with your known + unknown sides, rearrange (e.g. O = H sin θ).
- **Angle:** use **inverse** trig — θ = sin⁻¹/cos⁻¹/tan⁻¹ of the ratio.
- Always label O, A, H relative to the known angle first.
- Solves ladders, ramps, heights — any right-angled situation.

</Step>
