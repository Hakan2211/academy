<Step title="A fraction is a division waiting to happen" kind="explain">

A fraction like ¾ tells you two things: cut a whole into **4** equal parts (the **denominator**, below the line) and take **3** of them (the **numerator**, above). It's also just a division: ¾ = 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75.

The denominator names the *size* of the pieces; the numerator *counts* them.

</Step>

<Step title="See the pieces" kind="interactive">

Change how many pieces the whole is cut into, and how many you shade.

<FractionBar />

</Step>

<Step title="Equivalent fractions" kind="explain">

Cut each piece in half and you have twice as many pieces, each half the size — but the **shaded amount hasn't changed**. That's why:

<Formula tex="\frac{1}{2} = \frac{2}{4} = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{50}{100}" block />

Multiply (or divide) top and bottom by the *same* number and you get an **equivalent fraction**. Dividing until you can't go further gives the **simplest form** — the cleanest name for that amount.

</Step>

<Step title="Proper, improper, mixed" kind="explain">

- A **proper** fraction is less than one whole: ⅗.
- An **improper** fraction is one or more wholes: 7/4 (more pieces than make a whole).
- A **mixed number** writes that as wholes + a bit: 7/4 = 1¾.

They're all the same kinds of number — just dressed differently for the job at hand.

<Callout type="tip" title="Simplify by spotting common factors">
To simplify, divide top and bottom by their HCF. 18/24 → both share 6 → 3/4. The prime-factor skills from the last world pay off directly here.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- The **denominator** sets piece size; the **numerator** counts pieces.
- A fraction is also a **division**: a/b = a ÷ b.
- **Equivalent fractions** come from multiplying/dividing top and bottom by the same number.
- **Simplest form** divides out the HCF; improper fractions and mixed numbers are two views of the same value.

</Step>
