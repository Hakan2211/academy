<Step title="Per cent means per hundred" kind="explain">

"Per cent" is Latin for "per hundred." 45% literally means 45 out of every 100. That single idea makes percentages the universal language of comparison — test scores, interest rates, discounts, battery levels — because everything is rescaled to the same denominator of 100.

</Step>

<Step title="Finding a percentage of an amount" kind="explain">

To find a percentage of something, turn the percent into a fraction or decimal and multiply:

<Formula tex="30\% \text{ of } 80 = \frac{30}{100} \times 80 = 0.3 \times 80 = 24" block />

Mental shortcuts build from easy anchors: **10%** is ÷ 10, **1%** is ÷ 100, **50%** is half, **25%** is a quarter. Want 30%? Find 10% and triple it.

</Step>

<Step title="Percentages as a comparison tool" kind="explain">

19 out of 25 versus 30 out of 40 — which is better? Convert each to a percentage of 100:

<Formula tex="\frac{19}{25} = 76\% \qquad \frac{30}{40} = 75\%" block />

Now they're directly comparable. To express one number as a percentage of another: divide, then × 100.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Percent = per hundred**; it rescales everything to a denominator of 100.
- **% of an amount**: convert to a decimal/fraction and multiply.
- Anchors **10%, 1%, 50%, 25%** build any percentage mentally.
- To write one quantity as a % of another: **divide, then × 100**.

</Step>
