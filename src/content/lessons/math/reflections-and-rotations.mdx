<Step title="Flipping and turning" kind="explain">

Two more transformations that keep a shape's size but change how it sits:

- A **reflection** flips a shape across a **mirror line** — producing a mirror image (orientation reverses).
- A **rotation** turns a shape about a fixed **centre** by an angle (orientation preserved).

Both produce a **congruent** image — only the position and facing change.

</Step>

<Step title="Reflections" kind="explain">

To reflect, every point moves to the opposite side of the mirror line, the same distance away. Common mirror lines give simple coordinate rules:

<Formula tex="\text{in } x\text{-axis: } (x, y) \to (x, -y); \quad \text{in } y\text{-axis: } (x, y) \to (-x, y)" block />

Reflection in the line y = x swaps the coordinates: (x, y) → (y, x). A reflected shape is "handed" — like a left and right glove.

</Step>

<Step title="Rotations" kind="explain">

A rotation needs three things: a **centre**, an **angle**, and a **direction** (clockwise or anticlockwise). About the origin, the quarter-turns are clean:

<Formula tex="90° \text{ anticlockwise: } (x, y) \to (-y, x); \quad 180°: (x, y) \to (-x, -y)" block />

A 180° rotation is the same whichever way you turn. Unlike reflection, rotation keeps the shape's "handedness."

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- **Reflection** flips across a **mirror line** (orientation reverses): x-axis (x,−y), y-axis (−x,y), y=x swaps.
- **Rotation** turns about a **centre** by an angle (orientation kept): 90° ACW (−y,x), 180° (−x,−y).
- Both give a **congruent** image.
- Reflection changes "handedness"; rotation doesn't.

</Step>
