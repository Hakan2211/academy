<Step title="The deep idea behind shape" kind="explain">

Geometry isn't a list of facts to memorise — it's a **web of consequences**. From a few definitions, every property of every shape follows by logic. And the unifying thread running through it all is **symmetry**: the ways a shape can be moved and still look the same.

</Step>

</Step>

<Step title="Reflection and rotation" kind="explain">

- **Reflection symmetry:** a shape has a **line of symmetry** if folding along it matches the two halves. A square has 4 such lines; a circle has infinitely many.
- **Rotational symmetry:** the **order** is how many times a shape looks identical during one full turn. A square has order 4; an equilateral triangle order 3.

More symmetry means a more "regular" shape — and it explains why the regular polygons feel so balanced.

</Step>

<Step title="Symmetry explains properties" kind="explain">

Symmetry isn't decoration — it *causes* a shape's properties. An isosceles triangle has two equal base angles **because** it has a line of symmetry swapping them. A square's diagonals are equal and cross at right angles **because** its symmetry maps one onto the other. Spot the symmetry and the properties follow for free — no measuring needed.

<Callout type="info" title="Symmetry runs through all of science">
Symmetry is one of the most powerful ideas in mathematics and physics. Conservation of energy and momentum come from symmetries of time and space (Noether's theorem); the patterns of crystals, snowflakes, and even particle physics are classified by their symmetries. What starts as folding paper becomes a master principle of the universe.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step title="Tessellation: symmetry that fills space" kind="explain">

Some shapes tile the plane with no gaps — **tessellation**. Equilateral triangles, squares and regular hexagons each tessellate alone, because their angles divide evenly into 360° (hexagon: 3 × 120° = 360° around each point). It's why honeycomb is hexagonal — the most efficient way to fill space with equal cells. Shape, angle and symmetry meet in a bee's nest.

</Step>

<Step title="Recap" kind="recap">

- Geometry is a **logical web**; **symmetry** is its unifying thread.
- **Reflection** symmetry = mirror lines; **rotational** symmetry = order (matches per turn).
- Symmetry **causes** properties (isosceles base angles, square diagonals).
- Shapes **tessellate** when their angles divide into 360° — hence hexagonal honeycomb.

</Step>
